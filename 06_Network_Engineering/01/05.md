In the world of networking, **latency** and **jitter** are the two biggest enemies of a smooth experience. While they are
related, they represent two different types of delay.

---

## 1. Latency (The Delay)

Latency is the total time it takes for a piece of data (a packet) to travel from its source to its destination. It is often
measured in **milliseconds (ms)** and is commonly referred to as **"Ping."**

- **The Analogy:** Imagine you are at a restaurant. Latency is the time between you asking the waiter for a glass of water
  and the waiter actually placing that glass on your table.
- **What causes it?** Physical distance (the speed of light through fiber optics), the number of routers the data has to pass
  through, and network congestion.
- **Impact:** High latency causes "lag." In a video call, this looks like a long pause after you speak before the other
  person responds.

---

## 2. Jitter (The Variation)

Jitter is the **variation** in latency over time. It measures how inconsistent the arrival times of data packets are.

- **The Analogy:** Imagine you order three pizzas.
- If they arrive exactly 30 minutes apart, you have **high latency but low jitter** (predictable).
- If the first arrives in 10 minutes, the second in 40 minutes, and the third in 15 minutes, you have **high jitter**
  (unpredictable).

- **What causes it?** Network congestion that fluctuates, or packets taking different physical routes to reach the same
  destination.
- **Impact:** Jitter is the "killer" of real-time communication. It causes audio to sound robotic or "choppy" and video to
  skip frames or freeze.

---

## 3. Comparison Table

| Feature        | Latency                        | Jitter                                     |
| -------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Definition** | Total time for data to travel. | Variation in the time between packets.     |
| **Experience** | A constant "lag" or delay.     | Stuttering, choppiness, or buffering.      |
| **Good Score** | Under 50ms is ideal.           | Under 30ms is usually required for VoIP.   |
| **Analogy**    | The speed of the car.          | How much the car speeds up and slows down. |

---

## 4. How They Affect You

Different activities care about these metrics in different ways:

- **Web Browsing:** High latency is annoying (pages load slowly), but jitter doesn't really matter.
- **Netflix/Streaming:** High latency is okay (the video takes a second to start), and jitter is handled by "buffering"
  (downloading ahead of time).
- **Online Gaming & Video Calls:** Both must be low. High latency makes you feel "behind" the action, while high jitter makes
  the connection unstable and unusable.
