If you’re troubleshooting DNS or just curious about how a domain resolves, **nslookup** and **dig** are the two standard
tools in your Linux belt. While they both query DNS servers, they have very different personalities.

---

### 1. nslookup (Name Service Lookup)

Think of `nslookup` as the "classic" tool. It’s simple, cross-platform (works on Windows too), and operates in two modes:
interactive and non-interactive.

- **Best for:** Quick checks and people who prefer a simpler, less cluttered output.
- **Behavior:** It uses its own internal resolver library rather than the standard OS library, which can sometimes lead to
  slightly different results than what your browser sees.

**Common Commands:**

- **Basic lookup:** `nslookup google.com`
- **Specific Record (e.g., Mail):** `nslookup -query=mx google.com`
- **Using a specific DNS server:** `nslookup google.com 8.8.8.8`

---

### 2. dig (Domain Information Groper)

`dig` is the modern powerhouse. It is the successor to `nslookup` and is preferred by system administrators because it is
much more flexible and follows the DNS standards more strictly.

- **Best for:** Scripting, detailed troubleshooting, and seeing exactly what the DNS server sent back.
- **Behavior:** It outputs the full response from the server, including the header flags and timing information.

**Common Commands:**

- **Standard lookup:** `dig google.com`
- **Short answer (cleaner):** `dig google.com +short`
- **Querying specific types:** `dig google.com MX` or `dig google.com ANY`
- **Reverse lookup (IP to Name):** `dig -x 8.8.8.8`
- **Trace the path:** `dig google.com +trace` (This shows you every step from the Root servers down to the Authoritative
  server).

---

### Key Differences at a Glance

| Feature          | nslookup                            | dig                                    |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **Output Style** | Minimal and user-friendly.          | Verbose and technical.                 |
| **Scripting**    | Difficult to parse.                 | Designed for scripting (via `+short`). |
| **Information**  | Shows basic answers.                | Shows headers, flags, and TTL.         |
| **Reliability**  | Can be "chatty" with its own logic. | Direct reflection of the DNS response. |

### Which one should you use?

If you just want to know "What is the IP of this site?", **nslookup** is fine. However, if you are a Linux user or a dev
trying to figure out why a record hasn't updated or which specific name server is failing, **dig** is the superior tool. Most
Linux pros consider `nslookup` deprecated in favor of `dig`.
