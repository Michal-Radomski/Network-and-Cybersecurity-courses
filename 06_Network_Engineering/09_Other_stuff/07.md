**FIFO** stands for **First-In, First-Out**. It is a fundamental method of organizing and manipulating data (or physical
objects) where the oldest entry—the one that arrived first—is the first one to be processed or removed.

Think of it like a **queue at a coffee shop**: the first person to stand in line is the first person served, and they leave
the line before anyone who arrived after them.

---

### 1. FIFO in Computer Science (Data Structures)

In programming, FIFO is the governing principle of a **Queue**.

- **Enqueue:** Adding an element to the back of the line.
- **Dequeue:** Removing an element from the front of the line.

**Common uses include:**

- **Task Scheduling:** Operating systems use FIFO to manage processes waiting for CPU time.
- **IO Buffering:** When you type on a keyboard, the characters are stored in a FIFO buffer so they appear on the screen in
  the order you typed them.
- **Print Spoolers:** Documents sent to a printer are handled in the order they were received.

---

### 2. FIFO in Electronics (Hardware)

In digital logic and hardware engineering, a FIFO is a specialized memory buffer used to pass data between two systems that
might be running at different speeds (asynchronous communication).

- **Flow Control:** It prevents data loss by holding onto incoming bits until the receiving component is ready to process
  them.
- **Pipe Analogy:** Imagine water flowing through a pipe; the first drop of water to enter the pipe is the first drop to come
  out the other end.

---

### 3. FIFO in Finance and Accounting (Inventory)

In business, FIFO is a method used to value inventory. It assumes that the goods produced or purchased first are the ones
sold first.

- **Impact on Taxes:** During periods of inflation, FIFO results in a lower **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)** because you are
  "selling" your older, cheaper stock first. This leads to higher reported profits (and potentially higher taxes).
- **Perishable Goods:** This is the natural logical flow for businesses selling food or medicine to ensure products don't
  expire on the shelf.

---

### Summary Comparison: FIFO vs. LIFO

While FIFO is "First-In, First-Out," its opposite is **LIFO** (Last-In, First-Out).

| Feature            | FIFO (First-In, First-Out) | LIFO (Last-In, First-Out)    |
| ------------------ | -------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Analogy**        | A line at a bank.          | A stack of cafeteria trays.  |
| **Processing**     | Oldest items first.        | Newest items first.          |
| **Data Structure** | **Queue**                  | **Stack**                    |
| **Best For**       | Fairness and flow.         | Quick access to recent data. |
