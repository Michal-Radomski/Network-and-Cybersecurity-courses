Border Gateway Protocol (BGP) is essentially the **Postal Service of the Internet**. While protocols like OSPF or EIGRP
handle directions within a single company or building (Internal), BGP is what connects those buildings across the entire
globe.

It is a **Path Vector Protocol** that manages how packets are routed from one Autonomous System (AS) to another.

---

## 1. Autonomous Systems (AS)

The internet isn't one giant network; it’s a collection of over 100,000 smaller networks called **Autonomous Systems**.

- Each AS is a group of IP networks managed by a single entity (like Comcast, Google, or a University).
- Every AS is assigned a unique **ASN** (Autonomous System Number).
- BGP is the language these systems use to say, _"Hey, I have the best path to reach these specific IP addresses."_

---

## 2. Establishing Neighbor Relationships

BGP doesn't automatically "find" its neighbors. It relies on manual configuration.

1. **TCP Connection:** BGP uses **Port 179** to establish a reliable connection between two routers.
2. **Peering:** Once connected, the routers become "neighbors" or "peers."
3. **Exchange:** They exchange their entire routing tables initially, and then only send **incremental updates** when
   something changes.

There are two types of peering:

- **eBGP (External BGP):** Connecting two different Autonomous Systems.
- **iBGP (Internal BGP):** Connecting routers within the same Autonomous System.

---

## 3. The Best Path Selection

Unlike other protocols that look at "speed" (bandwidth) or "distance" (hops), BGP looks at **Path Attributes**. If there are
multiple ways to get to a destination, BGP runs them through a rigorous "tie-breaker" list.

The most famous attribute is the **AS_PATH**. BGP prefers the route that passes through the fewest number of Autonomous
Systems.

---

## 4. Why BGP is "Fragile"

BGP was built on **trust**. When a router tells the world, _"I own the IP addresses for Amazon.com,"_ other BGP routers
generally believe it.

- **BGP Hijacking:** If a network accidentally (or maliciously) claims to own IP addresses they don't, traffic can be
  redirected to the wrong place, leading to massive outages or security breaches.
- **Convergence:** Because the internet is so massive, it can take several minutes for a routing change to "propagate" or
  spread to every BGP router worldwide.

---

## Summary Table: BGP vs. Standard Routing

| Feature     | Interior Gateway Protocols (OSPF/RIP) | BGP (Path Vector)                     |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Scale**   | Small to Medium (Corporate)           | Massive (The whole Internet)          |
| **Goal**    | Fastest path to a destination         | Best path based on policy/business    |
| **Trust**   | Highly trusted                        | Trust-based (vulnerable to hijacking) |
| **Traffic** | Link-state or Distance-vector         | Path-vector (AS-by-AS)                |
