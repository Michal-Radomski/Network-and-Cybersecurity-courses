Since you are learning, the best way to visualize this is through the **OSI Model**. Think of sending a packet like sending a
physical letter through the mail. It doesn't just "teleport"; it gets wrapped in multiple layers of envelopes before it
leaves your house.

### 1. Sending: Encapsulation (The "Wrapping" Process)

When you click "Send" on an email, the data travels **down** the layers of your computer's software to the hardware.

- **Layer 7 (Application):** You write the message (e.g., "Hello!").
- **Layer 4 (Transport):** The computer breaks the message into **Segments** and adds a "Port Number" (like a specific
  apartment number) so the other side knows which app should open it.
- **Layer 3 (Network):** The segment is wrapped in an **IP Packet**. This adds the Source and Destination **IP Addresses**.
- **Layer 2 (Data Link):** The packet is wrapped in a **Frame**. This adds the **MAC Addresses** (physical hardware IDs) of
  your computer and your router.
- **Layer 1 (Physical):** The frame is converted into electrical pulses, light (fiber), or radio waves (Wi-Fi) and sent
  across the wire.

---

### 2. Receiving: De-encapsulation (The "Unwrapping" Process)

When the other computer receives those electrical pulses, it travels **up** the layers.

- **Check the Frame:** The computer looks at the MAC address. "Is this for me?" If yes, it peels off the Layer 2 wrapper.
- **Check the Packet:** It looks at the IP address. "Is this my IP?" If yes, it peels off the Layer 3 wrapper.
- **Check the Segment:** It looks at the Port Number. "Oh, this is for the Web Browser!" It peels off the Layer 4 wrapper.
- **Deliver Data:** The raw message ("Hello!") is finally handed to the application for you to read.

---

### 3. What a Packet Actually "Looks Like"

If you were to look at a single packet using a tool like Wireshark, it looks like a "Header" followed by the "Payload" (your
data).

| Section             | What's Inside       | Purpose                                                 |
| :------------------ | :------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Ethernet Header** | MAC Addresses       | Gets it to the next physical device (like your router). |
| **IP Header**       | IP Addresses        | Gets it to the final destination network.               |
| **TCP Header**      | Port Numbers        | Ensures the data is complete and goes to the right app. |
| **Data (Payload)**  | The actual "Hello!" | The information being moved.                            |

### Summary

- **Sending** is like putting a letter in an envelope, inside a box, inside a shipping container.
- **Receiving** is like opening the container, then the box, then the envelope to read the letter.
