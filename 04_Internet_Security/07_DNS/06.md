Once your browser has the IP address (the "what"), your computer needs to figure out the path (the "how"). This doesn't
happen in the DNS layer; it happens in the **Networking Layer**.

Your computer doesn't actually "know" the entire path to the website. It only knows who its **Next Hop** is.

---

## 1. The Routing Table (The Internal Map)

Your Operating System maintains a **Routing Table**. This is a list of rules that tells the computer which physical network
interface (Wi-Fi, Ethernet, VPN) to use based on the destination IP.

- **Local Traffic:** If the IP is on your home network (like a printer), the computer sends the signal directly to that
  device.
- **Internet Traffic (The Default Gateway):** For almost everything else, the routing table has a "Default Gateway" entry.
  This is usually the IP of your **Router**.

## 2. ARP: Finding the Hardware (MAC Address)

Even with an IP, your computer can't send a signal over a cable without a **MAC Address** (the unique serial number of a
physical network card).

- Your computer uses **ARP (Address Resolution Protocol)** to ask: _"Who has the IP address of my router? Tell me your
  physical MAC address."_
- The router responds, and your computer now knows exactly which physical "port" to push the electrons (or light) through.

## 3. BGP: The Internet's GPS

Once the signal hits your router and travels to your ISP, the decision-making moves to **BGP (Border Gateway Protocol)**.

Imagine the internet as a giant web of interconnected "islands" (Autonomous Systems). Your ISP’s routers look at the
destination IP and check their BGP tables to see which physical fiber-optic cable is the shortest or most reliable path to
the next island.

## 4. The Physical Choice

At every single router along the way, the same split-second decision is made:

1. **Look at the destination IP.**
2. **Check the Routing Table** for the best outbound path.
3. **Forward the packet** out of that specific physical cable (Ethernet, Fiber, or Satellite).

---

### Summary: The Layers of "Knowing"

| Level           | What it knows                          | Physical Choice                                                     |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Your PC**     | Knows the Router's IP.                 | Chooses Wi-Fi vs. Ethernet based on priority.                       |
| **Your Router** | Knows the ISP's connection.            | Sends signal through the Coax/Fiber line.                           |
| **The ISP**     | Knows the "map" of the internet (BGP). | Chooses the specific high-capacity undersea or cross-country cable. |

### How to see this in action

If you want to see exactly which "cables" (routers) your signal is choosing right now, open your terminal (Command Prompt or
Terminal) and type: `tracert google.com` (Windows) or `traceroute google.com` (Mac/Linux).

It will list every "hop" your signal takes to get to the destination!
