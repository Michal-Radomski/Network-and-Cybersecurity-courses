Securing your home router is like locking the front door to your digital life. You can block neighbors and hackers by
following these three core steps:

## 1. Update Your "Two Key Passwords"

Most people don't realize their router has two different passwords that both need changing immediately [1, 2] :

- The Wi-Fi Password: This is what you and your guests use to join the network. Use a long passphrase, like
  Blue-Coffee-Jump-2026, which is easy to remember but hard for hackers to crack [3] .
- The Router Admin Password: This is for the "control room" where you change settings. Hackers use default logins like
  admin/password to take over entire networks [4, 5] . Set a unique, complex password here that is different from your Wi-Fi
  password [6, 7] .

## 2. Use Modern Encryption (WPA3)

Encryption scrambles your data so even if a neighbor intercepts your signal, they can't read it [1, 8] .

- Enable WPA3: This is the current gold standard for 2026 [3, 9] .
- WPA2 as Backup: If you have older devices that can't use WPA3, use WPA2/WPA3 Mixed Mode (also called Transitional Mode)
  [10, 11] .
- Avoid WEP: Never use WEP or WPA (without a '2' or '3'); they are outdated and can be cracked in minutes [1, 10] .

## 3. Close the "Open Windows"

Certain "convenience" features are actually security holes that hackers love [1, 12] :

- Disable WPS: The "push-button" connection feature has major flaws that allow attackers to bypass your password [5, 9] .
- Turn off Remote Management: Ensure you can only change settings while physically at home, preventing someone from hacking
  your router over the internet [1, 5] .
- Set up a Guest Network: Create a separate network for visitors and smart home devices (IoT). This keeps them isolated so if
  a guest's phone has malware, it can't spread to your personal laptop [9] .
