When connecting to a new or public Wi-Fi network, keep this checklist handy to spot potential security threats before you log
in.

## Checklist: Wi-Fi Safety Red Flags

- Look-alike Network Names: Be wary of multiple networks with nearly identical names (e.g., Cafe_Guest vs. Cafe_Guest_Free)
  [1, 2] . This is often a sign of an "Evil Twin" hotspot set up to trick users [2, 3] .
- Unverified Generic Names: Avoid networks with broad, generic names like Free_Public_Wi-Fi or Airport_Network that aren't
  officially posted on signs or confirmed by staff [1, 3] .
- HTTP Instead of HTTPS: If a usually secure site (like your email or social media) loads as HTTP without the padlock icon,
  it may indicate a "Man-in-the-Middle" attack where encryption has been stripped away [1, 4, 5] .
- Requests for Software or Extensions: If a network asks you to install a special "login app," browser extension, or any
  extra software before you can connect, disconnect immediately [2, 6] .
- Suspicious Pop-ups or Ads: An unusual increase in pop-up ads or "System Alert" notifications asking for personal data after
  you connect can be a sign of a compromised network or malware injection [1, 7] .
- No Password Required: While convenient, completely open networks are significantly more vulnerable to eavesdropping and
  data sniffing than those requiring a password [2, 8, 9] .
- Performance Oddities: Persistent, unexplained disconnections or extremely slow speeds can sometimes be a byproduct of a
  hacker's device struggling to process your intercepted traffic [1, 10, 11] .

## Immediate Action Items

| If you see a Red Flag... | Do This Immediately                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Suspect a fake network   | Ask a staff member for the exact official network name [2, 3] .                                                            |
| Are forced to use it     | Enable a Reputable VPN[](https://surfshark.com/blog/risks-of-public-wifi) and confirm the "Kill Switch" is active [2, 3] . |
| Accidentally connected   | "Forget" the network in your device settings to prevent automatic reconnection [2, 3] .                                    |
| See browser warnings     | Do not ignore certificate error messages or "Not Secure" warnings [2] .                                                    |
