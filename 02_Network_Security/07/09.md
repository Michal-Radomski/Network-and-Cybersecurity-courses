Aircrack-ng is not a single tool, but a complete suite of command-line utilities used for auditing and assessing wireless
network security. The process of "cracking" typically involves using several of these tools in a specific sequence to exploit
weaknesses in WEP or WPA/WPA2-PSK encryption.

## The Standard Cracking Workflow

Most modern security audits follow these core steps to capture the data needed for an offline crack:

1.  Monitor Mode (airmon-ng): First, the wireless adapter is placed into "monitor mode". This allows the card to "listen" to
    all wireless traffic in the air, rather than just data addressed specifically to your device.
2.  Target Reconnaissance (airodump-ng): This tool scans the airwaves to list nearby networks, their BSSIDs (MAC addresses),
    channels, and encryption types. You use it to identify the specific target and see which clients are currently connected
    to it.
3.  Capturing the Handshake: For WPA/WPA2 networks, the goal is to capture a 4-way handshake, which is the encrypted
    "greeting" exchanged when a device connects to a router.

- Deauthentication (aireplay-ng): Instead of waiting for someone to log in, you can send "deauth" packets to force a
  connected device to disconnect. When that device automatically reconnects, airodump-ng captures the resulting 4-way
  handshake and saves it to a file.

4.  Offline Cracking (aircrack-ng): Once you have the handshake file, you run it through the aircrack-ng tool alongside a
    wordlist (a dictionary of millions of potential passwords). The tool quickly tests every password in the list against the
    captured handshake until it finds a match.

## How WEP vs. WPA Cracking Differs

- WEP (Legacy): Relies on capturing a high volume of data packets to exploit statistical weaknesses in the encryption. It can
  often be cracked in minutes because the more traffic you generate (using aireplay-ng to "inject" packets), the faster the
  password is revealed.
- WPA/WPA2-PSK: Security depends entirely on the strength of the password. Since the actual cracking happens offline using
  the captured handshake, it cannot be "speed up" by the router's hardware; it is purely limited by how fast your computer
  can guess passwords from your wordlist.

## Ethical & Legal Note

Using these tools to access networks you do not own or have explicit permission to test is illegal in most jurisdictions.
These methods should only be used for authorized security assessments and learning purposes.
