Monitoring and analysis are two sides of the same coin: **Monitoring** tells you _what_ is happening (availability and
health), while **Analysis** tells you _why_ it’s happening (security threats or performance bottlenecks).

Think of monitoring as the dashboard in your car, and analysis as the mechanic plugging in a diagnostic tool.

---

## 1. Network Traffic Monitoring (The "What")

Monitoring is the continuous process of observing a network for slow or failing components. It focuses on high-level metrics
like bandwidth usage, uptime, and error rates.

### How to do it:

- **Establish a Baseline:** You can’t tell what’s "weird" if you don’t know what’s "normal." Monitor your network for a week
  to understand typical peak hours and data loads.
- **Use SNMP (Simple Network Management Protocol):** Most hardware (routers/switches) uses SNMP to report their status to a
  central server.
- **Set Up Alerts:** Configure thresholds. For example, if CPU usage on a core switch hits 90% or a link reaches 80%
  capacity, you should get an immediate notification.

### Key Metrics to Watch:

- **Throughput:** The actual amount of data being moved.
- **Packet Loss:** If packets are dropping, your hardware or cables might be failing.
- **Latency:** The delay in data transfer (the "lag" factor).

---

## 2. Network Traffic Analysis (The "Why")

Analysis involves "Deep Packet Inspection" (DPI). You aren't just looking at the volume of traffic; you are looking inside
the data packets to see where they are going and what they contain.

### How to do it:

- **Capture Traffic:** Use a tool like **Wireshark** or **tcpdump** to "sniff" the wire. You’ll need a **SPAN port** (Mirror
  port) on your switch or a **Network TAP** to copy the traffic to your analysis machine.
- **Flow Analysis (NetFlow/SFlow):** Instead of looking at every single packet (which is data-heavy), flow analysis looks at
  "conversations" between IP addresses. It’s like looking at a phone bill to see who called whom and for how long.
- **Protocol Identification:** Analyze which protocols are hogging your bandwidth. Is it legitimate SQL traffic, or is
  someone running BitTorrent in the office?

## The Essential Toolkit

| Tool Type               | Popular Examples          | Best Use Case                                       |
| :---------------------- | :------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------- |
| **Monitors**            | PRTG, Zabbix, SolarWinds  | Tracking uptime and device health.                  |
| **Analyzers**           | Wireshark, Zeek           | Investigating security breaches or bugs.            |
| **Flow Collectors**     | Scrutinizer, ManageEngine | Seeing high-level traffic patterns and top talkers. |
| **Intrusion Detection** | Snort, Suricata           | Automatically flagging "malicious-looking" traffic. |

---

## A Simple Workflow for Success

1.  **Mirror the Traffic:** Configure your switch to send a copy of all traffic to a dedicated monitoring port.
2.  **Filter Heavily:** Analysis generates massive amounts of data. Use filters to ignore "noise" (like standard background
    updates) and focus on specific IPs or ports.
3.  **Visualize:** Use tools like **Grafana** or **Elasticsearch (ELK Stack)** to turn raw data logs into readable charts.
    It’s much easier to spot a spike on a graph than a line in a text file.

---

### A Word on Privacy and Ethics

Monitoring involves looking at data that doesn't belong to you. In a professional environment, ensure you have explicit
authorization and that your activities comply with local privacy laws (like GDPR).
