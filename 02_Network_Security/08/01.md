Sniffing and spoofing are two fundamental techniques used in network attacks, often working together to compromise your data.
While sniffing is about silently listening, spoofing is about active deception [1, 2, 3] .

## Sniffing: The Digital Eavesdropper

Sniffing is the process of intercepting and capturing data packets as they travel across a network [4, 5] .

- How it works: Attackers use software called "packet sniffers" (like [Wireshark](https://www.wireshark.org/)) to capture
  unencrypted data like usernames, passwords, and emails [4, 6, 7] .
- Passive Sniffing: The attacker simply "listens" to traffic passing through a hub or a wireless access point without
  interacting with the network [8, 9] .
- Active Sniffing: The attacker actively injects traffic or manipulates network protocols (like ARP poisoning) to force data
  to be sent to their machine [2, 6] .

## Spoofing: The Digital Impersonator

Spoofing occurs when an attacker disguises their identity to appear as a trusted source [5, 10] .

- How it works: By faking identifiers like IP addresses or MAC addresses, an attacker tricks a system into believing they are
  a legitimate user or server [2, 11] .
- Common Types:
- IP Spoofing: Creating IP packets with a forged source address to impersonate another computer [11, 12] .
  - DNS Spoofing: Redirecting users to a malicious website by giving them false domain name information [12, 13] .
  - MAC Spoofing: Changing a device's hardware address to bypass network security filters [11, 14] .

## The "Tag Team" Attack

Hackers frequently use spoofing to make sniffing more effective [2] . For example, in an ARP spoofing attack, an attacker
spoofs the identity of your router. Your device then mistakenly sends all its private data directly to the attacker's
machine, where they can "sniff" it at their leisure [15, 16] .

| Feature      | Sniffing                                  | Spoofing                                      |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Primary Goal | Intercepting data (Eavesdropping) [5, 17] | Impersonating a source (Deception) [1, 5]     |
| Action Type  | Passive or Active [9]                     | Always Active [2]                             |
| Main Defense | Strong Encryption (WPA3/HTTPS) [4, 9]     | Digital Signatures & Network Filtering [3, 4] |
