Pharming is a deceptive cyberattack that automatically redirects you from a legitimate website to a fraudulent one, even if
you typed the correct web address into your browser. It is often described as "phishing without a lure" because it doesn't
require you to click a malicious link in an email to work. [1, 2, 3, 4] The primary goal of a pharmer is to steal sensitive
information like login credentials, credit card numbers, and other personal data by tricking you into entering it on a
"spoofed" (fake) website that looks identical to the real one. [5, 6]

## How Pharming Works

Pharming exploits the Domain Name System (DNS), the internet's "phonebook" that translates human-readable URLs (like
://bank.com) into numerical IP addresses. Attackers use two main methods: [1, 7]

- DNS Cache Poisoning: This is a broad attack where hackers corrupt a DNS server's temporary records. Once "poisoned," the
  server sends thousands of users to the wrong IP address simultaneously, regardless of whether their own computers are
  infected.
- Malware-based Pharming: This targets individual devices. A Trojan or virus is installed on your computer (often via a
  suspicious download) and modifies your local hosts file. This file contains local IP mappings that your computer checks
  before asking a DNS server; by changing it, the attacker forces your browser to the fake site every time. [1, 2, 3, 8, 9,
  10]

## Pharming vs. Phishing

While both aim to steal data, they differ in their execution: [3, 4, 11]

- Phishing relies on social engineering to trick you into taking an action, such as clicking a link in a fake email.
- Pharming manipulates the underlying internet infrastructure, meaning you can be compromised just by trying to visit your
  usual, trusted site. [12, 13, 14, 15]

## How to Protect Yourself

- Use HTTPS: Always check for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar. Pharming sites often lack valid SSL
  certificates.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Tools like Google Authenticator provide a second layer of defense, ensuring that
  even if a pharmer steals your password, they can't access your account.
- Secure Your Router: Change default administrator passwords on your home router to prevent hackers from hijacking your local
  DNS settings.
- Use Secure DNS Services: Consider using reputable DNS providers like
  [Cloudflare DNS (1.1.1.1)](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/what-is-dns/) or Google Public DNS, which offer better
  security against poisoning attacks. [1, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20]
