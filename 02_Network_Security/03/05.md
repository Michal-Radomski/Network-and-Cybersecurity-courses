Both rootkits and spyware are types of malware designed to hide on your system, but they have very different jobs once they
get inside.

## 1. Spyware (The Digital Stalker)

Spyware is software that secretly monitors your activity and sends that information to a third party.

- Goal: To steal information (passwords, credit card numbers, browsing habits).
- How it works: It often hitches a ride on "free" software or malicious email attachments. Once installed, it can log your
  keystrokes (keyloggers), take screenshots, or even turn on your webcam.
- Visibility: You might notice a slow computer, constant pop-up ads, or your browser's home page changing without your
  permission.

## 2. Rootkits (The Invisible Master)

A rootkit is a collection of tools designed to grant an attacker "root" (administrative) access to a computer while hiding
its presence.

- Goal: To maintain long-term, undetected control over a system.
- How it works: Rootkits are incredibly dangerous because they "live" deep within the Operating System (sometimes even in the
  hardware firmware). They modify system files so that when you ask your computer, "Which programs are running?", the rootkit
  tells the OS to hide the attacker's tools from the list.
- Visibility: Almost invisible. Standard Task Managers or basic Antivirus programs often cannot see them because the rootkit
  has lied to the system itself.

## Key Differences

| Feature     | Spyware                            | Rootkit                             |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Primary Aim | Steal data / Monitor activity      | Maintain hidden "Root" control      |
| Location    | Application level (User space)     | System level (Kernel/Firmware)      |
| Detection   | Easier (visible performance drops) | Extremely difficult (hides from OS) |
| Removal     | Standard malware scanners          | Often requires a full OS reinstall  |

## How to protect your Ubuntu system

Since you are on Ubuntu, you have access to specific tools to hunt for these:

- For Spyware: Be cautious with PPA repositories and only install software from trusted sources.
- For Rootkits: Use a tool called Chkrootkit or RKHunter (Rootkit Hunter).
- To install: sudo apt install rkhunter chkrootkit
  - To run a scan: sudo rkhunter --check
