In cybersecurity, CIAAN is an expanded version of the traditional CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability).
It adds two critical pillars to address the complexities of modern digital transactions and identity verification. [1, 2, 3,
4] The acronym stands for:

- Confidentiality: Ensuring that sensitive information is accessible only to those authorized to have it.
- Integrity: Guaranteeing that data is accurate and has not been tampered with or modified during storage or transmission.
- Availability: Ensuring that systems and data are consistently accessible to authorized users when needed.
- Authenticity: Verifying that the identities of users or the origins of data and communications are genuine and trustworthy.
- Non-repudiation: Providing proof that a specific action or transaction occurred so that the sender or receiver cannot later
  deny their involvement. [5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11]

## Why the expansion?

While the original CIA Triad was sufficient for isolated systems, the rise of e-business and global networking necessitated
stronger proof of identity (Authenticity) and accountability (Non-repudiation). Together, these five pillars form a more
comprehensive foundation for securing modern information systems. [12, 13, 14]
