The main difference between the two is their role in a cyberattack: spoofing is the disguise used to build trust, while
phishing is the actual crime of stealing your data. [1, 2] While they are often used together, they differ in their methods
and goals:

## Key Differences at a Glance

| Feature [2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10] | Spoofing                                                                       | Phishing                                                                            |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary Goal                         | To impersonate a trusted source.                                               | To steal information or money.                                                      |
| Method                               | Technical manipulation of identifiers (e.g., email headers, IP addresses).     | Social engineering and psychological manipulation (e.g., creating urgency or fear). |
| Interaction                          | Can happen in the background without user interaction (e.g., IP/DNS spoofing). | Requires the victim to take an action, such as clicking a link or replying.         |
| Detection                            | Hard to spot without technical analysis of headers or protocols.               | Easier to spot through "red flags" like poor grammar or urgent threats.             |

## How They Work Together

In a typical 2026-era scam, spoofing sets the stage for phishing: [1, 3]

1.  The Spoof (The Costume): An attacker fakes a sender's email address to look like it's from your CEO or your bank's
    official domain.
2.  The Phish (The Con): The email uses the trust created by the fake address to pressure you into clicking a link to "verify
    your account" on a malicious page. [2, 10, 11]

## Modern Trends in 2026

- AI-Powered Impersonation: Attackers now use
  [Generative AI](https://www.sharp.co.uk/news-and-events/blog/hyper-realistic-phishing-deepfake-attacks-in-2026) to create
  hyper-personalized messages and deepfake voice clones for more convincing vishing (voice phishing) attacks.
- Targeting Authentication: Rather than just stealing passwords, modern phishing often aims for session tokens and
  multi-factor authentication (MFA) bypass to gain full control of an identity. [12, 13, 14, 15]
