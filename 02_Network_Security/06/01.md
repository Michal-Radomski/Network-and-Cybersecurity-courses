At its core, a **firewall** is a network security device that monitors incoming and outgoing network traffic. It decides
whether to allow or block specific traffic based on a defined set of security rules.

Think of it as a high-tech security guard stationed at the "gate" of your network (or your individual computer), checking the
credentials of every data packet that tries to pass through.

---

## How a Firewall Works

A firewall establishes a barrier between a trusted internal network and untrusted external networks, such as the internet. It
inspects **packets**—small chunks of data used for communication—to see if they meet the "safety" criteria you've
established.

---

## Common Types of Firewalls

Firewalls have evolved significantly from simple filters to complex systems that can "see" deep into the data they are
protecting.

### 1. Packet Filtering Firewall

The oldest and most basic type. It acts at the network layer and checks a packet's basic information: its source and
destination IP addresses, protocol, and port number.

- **Pros:** Very fast and efficient.
- **Cons:** It doesn't look at the actual content of the data, making it easy to bypass with sophisticated attacks.

### 2. Circuit-Level Gateway

These verify the **TCP handshake**—the digital "introduction" between two systems. Once the connection is deemed legitimate,
the gateway allows traffic to flow without further inspection.

- **Pros:** Resource-efficient.
- **Cons:** Like packet filtering, it doesn't inspect the data itself; if a connection is "valid" but contains malware, it
  gets through.

### 3. Stateful Inspection Firewall

A step up in intelligence, these track the "state" of active connections. They remember previous packets and only allow
incoming traffic if it is a response to an outbound request you actually made.

- **Pros:** Much more secure than simple packet filtering.
- **Cons:** Slightly more resource-intensive.

### 4. Application-Level Gateway (Proxy Firewall)

This acts as an intermediary. When you access a website, the proxy connects to the site for you, inspects the data at the
**application layer** (like HTTP or FTP), and then passes it to you.

- **Pros:** Highest level of security; masks your internal network ID.
- **Cons:** Can significantly slow down network performance.

### 5. Next-Generation Firewall (NGFW)

The modern gold standard. NGFWs combine traditional firewall features with advanced capabilities like:

- **Deep Packet Inspection (DPI):** Looking at the actual data inside the packet.
- **Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS):** Actively stopping attacks in real-time.
- **Malware Filtering:** Blocking known viruses and threats.

---

## Deployment Styles

Firewalls aren't just hardware boxes; they come in different "flavors" depending on where they live:

| Type                  | Description                                                                        |
| :-------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Software Firewall** | Installed on individual devices (like the Windows Firewall on your PC).            |
| **Hardware Firewall** | A physical device placed between your router and the internet.                     |
| **Cloud Firewall**    | Provided as a service (FWaaS), great for scaling and protecting remote workforces. |
