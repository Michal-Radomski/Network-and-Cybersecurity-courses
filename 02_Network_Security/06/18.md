Most modern "Total Security" or "Premium" suites for Windows include some form of Deep Packet Inspection (DPI), though they
often market it under proprietary names like "Network Attack Defense" or "Advanced Web Protection." [1, 2]

## Top Windows Suites with DPI Features

The following suites are recognized for their ability to inspect the actual data payload of network traffic: [3]

- [Bitdefender Total Security](https://www.bitdefender.com/en-us/consumer/total-security): Uses Network Attack Defense (NAD),
  which is a dedicated DPI solution designed to detect and block complex network exploits before they reach the OS.
- Kaspersky Premium: Includes advanced DPI technology that analyzes industrial and standard network traffic for anomalies. It
  is frequently cited as having one of the most robust network inspection engines in consumer software.
- [Norton 360 Deluxe/Premium](https://pl.norton.com/): Features an Intelligent Firewall that uses deep inspection to monitor
  two-way traffic and block unauthorized access attempts. It also utilizes AI-powered scam detection to analyze data within
  web links and emails in real-time.
- ESET Smart Security Premium: While the standard Internet Security version covers network protection, the Premium and
  Ultimate tiers leverage deeper SSL/TLS scanning to inspect encrypted traffic for hidden threats.
- Sophos Home Premium: Based on their enterprise-grade technology, Sophos provides a "next-gen" firewall experience for
  Windows that includes proactive threat monitoring and deep packet inspection specifically designed to stop ransomware and
  zero-day exploits. [1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]

## Why these suites are "Next-Gen"

Standard Windows Security is a solid Stateful firewall (Generations 1 & 2), but these paid suites elevate your protection to
Generations 4 and 5 by adding: [9]

- SSL/TLS Decryption: They can temporarily "unzip" encrypted traffic (like HTTPS) to scan the actual contents for malware
  before re-encrypting it.
- Behavioral Analysis: They don't just look for "bad files"; they look for "bad behavior" in the data stream (e.g., a file
  suddenly trying to encrypt your documents).
- IPS/IDS: They include Intrusion Prevention Systems that recognize known hacker "signatures" inside data packets. [1, 6, 8,
  10, 11]
