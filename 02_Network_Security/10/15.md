In the context of networking and tools like NSAuditor or Nmap, these "states" refer to the status of a TCP connection. TCP is
a connection-oriented protocol, meaning it follows a specific lifecycle from start to finish. Here are the most common states
you will see:

## 1. LISTENING

The service (like a web server or SSH) is open and "waiting" for a client to connect. It is not currently talking to anyone,
but it is ready to receive a request.

- Analogy: A receptionist sitting at a desk waiting for the phone to ring.

## 2. ESTABLISHED

A successful connection has been made. The "Three-Way Handshake" (SYN, SYN-ACK, ACK) is complete, and data is currently being
exchanged between your computer and the remote host.

- Analogy: You are actively talking to someone on the phone.

## 3. TIME_WAIT

The connection has been closed by your side, but your computer keeps the port "reserved" for a short period (usually 30–120
seconds). This ensures any delayed data packets still traveling through the internet are ignored and don't accidentally
interfere with a new connection.

- Analogy: Hanging up the phone but keeping your hand on the receiver for a second to make sure the line is actually clear.

## 4. CLOSE_WAIT

The remote side has closed the connection, and your computer is waiting for the local application (like your browser) to
acknowledge and close its end.

- Note: If you see too many of these, it often means an application is "hanging" or buggy.

## 5. SYN_SENT

Your computer has sent a request to start a connection (a SYN packet) and is waiting for the other side to say "Hello" back.

- Analogy: You’ve dialed a number and are waiting for the other person to pick up.

## 6. FIN_WAIT (1 & 2)

## The connection is in the process of being terminated. One side has sent a "Finished" (FIN) signal and is waiting for the final handshake to end the session.

## How to see these on your laptop right now:

You don't even need NSAuditor for a quick look. Open your Command Prompt or Terminal and type: netstat -an This will show you
a list of every active connection and its current State.
