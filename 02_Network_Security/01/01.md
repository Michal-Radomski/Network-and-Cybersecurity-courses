A computer network is a system of two or more interconnected computing devices that communicate and share resources with each
other. These devices, often called nodes, can include computers, servers, smartphones, and printers. [1, 2, 3, 4]

## Core Components

Networks are built from two main building blocks: [5]

- Links: The physical or wireless connections between devices, such as Ethernet cables, fiber optics, or Wi-Fi signals.
- Nodes/Hardware: Specialized devices that manage data flow, including:
- Switches: Connect multiple devices within a single local network.
  - Routers: Act as "traffic officers," directing data between different networks and connecting them to the internet.
  - Firewalls: Security devices that monitor traffic to block unauthorized access. [1, 3, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10]

## How They Work

Devices on a network follow standardized rules called protocols (like TCP/IP) to ensure data is transmitted accurately. When
you send information, it is broken into small packets, labeled with a destination IP address, and sent along the most
efficient path to the recipient. [3, 6, 7, 11]

## Common Types of Networks

Networks are often categorized by the geographical area they cover: [12]

- PAN (Personal Area Network): Connects personal devices within a very short range, usually for one person (e.g., Bluetooth
  earbuds to a phone).
- LAN (Local Area Network): Connects devices in a limited area like a home, office, or school.
- MAN (Metropolitan Area Network): Spans a larger area, such as a city or a large campus.
- WAN (Wide Area Network): Covers vast distances, such as countries or continents; the Internet is the largest example of a
  WAN. [2, 4, 7, 13, 14, 15, 16]
