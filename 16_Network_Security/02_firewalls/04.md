Configuring a third-party firewall like **ESET** or **Avast** gives you much deeper control over your network traffic than
the standard Windows Firewall. Because these suites are designed to be "smart," they usually work well out of the box, but
you can dramatically increase your security by applying a few "golden rules" of network defense.

---

## 1. The Golden Rules of Firewall Configuration

When configuring any firewall, keeping these core security principles in mind will prevent both security gaps and accidental
self-lockouts:

- **The Principle of Least Privilege (Default Deny):** Your ultimate goal should be to block _all_ incoming and outgoing
  traffic by default, and only allow specific, trusted applications to communicate. While blocking all outgoing traffic takes
  time to configure, it is the most secure setup.
- **Segregate Your Network Profiles:** Always treat **Public Wi-Fi** and **Home/Work (Private) Networks** differently. On
  public networks, your firewall should make your device completely invisible (stealth mode) and block all unsolicited
  incoming traffic.
- **Trust the "Interactive" or "Training" Mode First:** Instead of guessing which ports your apps need, switch your firewall
  to **Interactive Mode** (or "Learning Mode") for a few days. The firewall will prompt you with a pop-up every time an app
  tries to connect, allowing you to create permanent "Allow" or "Block" rules based on your actual daily usage.
- **Keep Rules Specific, Not Broad:** When creating a custom rule, don't just "Allow all traffic" for a program. Restrict it
  to the specific **port** (e.g., port 443 for web browsers) and **IP address** it actually needs to communicate with.

## 2. How to Configure ESET Personal Firewall

ESET is highly praised for its granular control. By default, it runs in **Automatic mode**, which allows most outbound
traffic and blocks unsolicited inbound traffic.

### Step-by-Step Configuration:

1. Open your ESET program and go to **Setup** > **Network protection**.
2. Click the gear icon next to **Firewall** and select **Configure...** (or go to **Advanced Setup** > **Protections** >
   **Network access protection** > **Firewall**).
3. **Change the Filtering Mode:**

- **Automatic Mode (Default):** Good for beginners, but not the most secure.
- **Interactive Mode:** Highly recommended for advanced users. It prompts you for every new connection. You can check
  "Remember action" to build your rule list over a week.
- **Policy-based Mode:** Blocks all connections that do not have a specific rule written for them.

4. **Enable Stealth Mode (IDS and Advanced Options):**

- Under Network Protection, ensure **Network attack protection (IDS)** and **Botnet protection** are enabled. This blocks
  hackers trying to scan your computer for open ports.

5. **Establish Rules:**

- Under the _Rules_ section, click **Edit**. You can manually block specific applications (like telemetry tools or old
  software) from ever reaching the internet.

## 3. How to Configure Avast Premium Security Firewall

Avast’s firewall is highly automated but offers an excellent, user-friendly interface to restrict application behaviors.

### Step-by-Step Configuration:

1. Open Avast and go to **Protection** > **Firewall**.
2. **Set your Network Profile correctly:**

- If you are at home, make sure the network is set to **Private** (trusted).
- If you are on public Wi-Fi, change it to **Public** (untrusted). This instantly hides your computer from other devices on
  the network.

3. **Configure Application Rules:**

- Click on **Application rules** (or the Settings gear > Firewall).
- Here you will see a list of all apps on your PC. You can use the sliders to decide exactly how much access an app has.
- **Pro-Tip:** Set unrecognized apps to **Block** or **Ask** to prevent unauthorized background data transmission.

4. **Enable Leak Protection (Under Settings):**

- Make sure **ARP Spoofing Defence** and **Port Scan Detection** are turned on. These prevent local network attacks from
  other compromised devices on your router.

---

## 4. Summary Checklist for a Secure Setup

| Feature                             | Recommended Setting    | Why?                                                                                         |
| ----------------------------------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Network Profile (Public)**        | **Strict / Untrusted** | Prevents other devices on public Wi-Fi from seeing or attacking your PC.                     |
| **IDS / IPS (Intrusion Detection)** | **Enabled**            | Actively blocks active hacking attempts, port scans, and brute-force attacks.                |
| **Interactive/Ask Mode**            | **On (Temporary)**     | Allows you to audit exactly what your apps are doing in the background.                      |
| **Notifications**                   | **Enabled**            | Alerts you immediately if a new, potentially malicious program tries to access the internet. |
