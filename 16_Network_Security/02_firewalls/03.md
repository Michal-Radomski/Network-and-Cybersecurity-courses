Setting up the built-in Windows Defender Firewall is one of the best ways to secure your computer. Windows includes a highly
effective, stateful firewall by default, but you can tweak its settings to make it even more secure.

Here is a step-by-step guide to securing your Windows Firewall.

---

### Step 1: Ensure the Firewall is Turned On

First, verify that the firewall is active for all network profiles (Domain, Private, and Public).

1. Press the **Windows Key**, type **Control Panel**, and press Enter.
2. Click on **System and Security** > **Windows Defender Firewall**.
3. On the left menu, click **Turn Windows Defender Firewall on or off**.
4. Select **Turn on Windows Defender Firewall** for both Private and Public network settings.
5. Check the box that says **"Block all incoming connections, including those in the list of allowed apps"** for the **Public
   network settings** (this provides maximum security when you are on public Wi-Fi like at a coffee shop or airport).
6. Click **OK**.

---

### Step 2: Restrict Allowed Apps (Inbound Traffic)

By default, Windows allows certain applications to accept incoming connections. You should audit this list and remove
anything you don't recognize or use.

1. In the main Windows Defender Firewall window, click **Allow an app or feature through Windows Defender Firewall** on the
   left.
2. Click the **Change settings** button at the top right (you may need administrator permission).
3. Review the list of apps:

- **Private checkmark:** Allows the app to receive data when you are on a trusted home/work network.
- **Public checkmark:** Allows the app to receive data on public networks (keep this as minimal as possible).

4. Uncheck any apps or features you do not use (e.g., File and Printer Sharing, Remote Desktop, or old games if you no longer
   play them).
5. Click **OK**.

---

### Step 3: Advance Security – Block All Outbound Traffic (Optional)

By default, Windows Firewall allows all _outbound_ traffic (data leaving your computer). If a piece of malware infects your
system, it can freely communicate with its server. For a highly secure setup, you can change the firewall to block all
outbound traffic except for apps you explicitly approve.

> ⚠️ **Note:** This is an advanced security measure. Enabling this will initially block your web browsers, games, and Windows
> Update until you manually create "allow" rules for them.

To configure advanced rules:

1. Press the **Windows Key**, type **Windows Defender Firewall with Advanced Security**, and press Enter.
2. In the center pane, click on **Windows Defender Firewall Properties**.
3. Look at the tabs for **Domain Profile**, **Private Profile**, and **Public Profile**:

- Change **Outbound connections** from _Allow (default)_ to **Block**.

4. Click **Apply** and **OK**.

If you do this, you will need to go to **Outbound Rules** on the left menu, click **New Rule...** on the right, and manually
select the programs (like `chrome.exe` or `msedge.exe`) that you want to allow to connect to the internet.

---

### Step 4: Keep Network Profiles Accurate

Windows will ask you "Do you want to allow your PC to be discoverable by other PCs and devices on this network?" whenever you
connect to a new Wi-Fi network.

- Choose **Yes** only for your trusted home network (**Private** profile).
- Choose **No** for any friends' houses, schools, or public spaces (**Public** profile). This instantly locks down the
  firewall to its strictest settings.
