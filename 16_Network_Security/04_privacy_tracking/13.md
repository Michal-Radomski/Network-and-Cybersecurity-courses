When customizing Firefox for privacy, a critical rule of thumb is **less is more**. If you install a massive list of ten
different extensions, your browser will actually stand out _more_ to trackers, increasing your digital fingerprint.

Instead of stacking extensions, pair Firefox's built-in **Strict Enhanced Tracking Protection** with a few open-source,
highly trusted extensions.

---

## 1. The Core Essential (Must Have)

### uBlock Origin

If you only install one single extension, make it **uBlock Origin**. Do not confuse it with "uBlock" or other ad
blockers—this is a free, open-source, wide-spectrum content blocker.

- **What it does:** It completely blocks ads, third-party tracking scripts, malware-serving domains, and pop-ups. It is
  highly optimized, meaning it speeds up your page load times and consumes very little computer memory.
- **Pro Tip:** In its settings under "Filter lists," you can check the box for **AdGuard URL Tracking Protection** to
  automatically strip out tracking junk from web links.

---

## 2. The Optional "Deep Privacy" Layer

If you want to take your privacy a step further without bogging down your browser, these specific tools solve unique tracking
issues:

### ClearURLs

When you copy or click links on social media or search engines, they often look like long, messy strings of code (e.g.,
`website.com/page?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=tracker...`).

- **What it does:** It silently and instantly scrubs those tracking elements out of URLs, leaving you with clean, untrackable
  links.

### Firefox Multi-Account Containers (Built by Mozilla)

This is an official tool that lets you separate your online identity into completely isolated, color-coded tabs (like "Work,"
"Shopping," or "Personal").

- **What it does:** Cookies downloaded in one container tab cannot see or communicate with cookies in another. For example,
  if you log into Facebook inside a designated container tab, Facebook's scripts are trapped there and cannot track what you
  are doing in your other standard browsing tabs.

### Cookie AutoDelete

- **What it does:** While Firefox natively handles cookie blocking well, this extension lets you take absolute control. It
  automatically deletes any cookies and cached data from a specific tab the exact moment you close it, unless you have
  specifically added that website to your "whitelist" (like your email or banking site).

---

## 3. Advanced Security (For Experienced Users)

### NoScript

- **What it does:** This blocks all JavaScript, Flash, and other executable code from running on web pages by default.
- **The Catch:** JavaScript runs almost the entire modern internet. If you turn this extension on, almost every website you
  visit will break instantly until you manually whitelist it. It provides bulletproof security but requires constant
  tweaking.

---

### A Recommendation Checklist

For an easy, highly protected, and fast daily browsing experience:

1. Turn Firefox’s native tracking protection to **Strict** (in Settings > Privacy & Security).
2. Install **uBlock Origin**.
3. Install **ClearURLs**.

Stop there. This minimal setup covers roughly 95% of online trackers without slowing down your computer or breaking the
websites you use every day.
