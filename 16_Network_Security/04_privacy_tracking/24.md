**Firefox Hardening** refers to modifying Mozilla Firefox's default settings and configuration files to maximize your
privacy, boost security, and block tracking.

Out of the box, Firefox is more privacy-friendly than Google Chrome, but it still has features enabled—like telemetry,
default search partnerships, and WebRTC—that can expose your data.

---

## Step 1: Basic GUI Settings (Easy)

Before diving into advanced settings, make these quick adjustments directly in the normal Firefox settings menu (**Settings >
Privacy & Security**):

- **Enhanced Tracking Protection:** Change this from _Standard_ to **Strict**. This blocks known fingerprinters,
  cryptominers, and cross-site trackers.
- **Website Privacy Preferences:** Check the boxes for **"Send websites a 'Do Not Track' request"** and **"Tell websites not
  to sell or share my data."**
- **Firefox Data Collection and Use:** **Uncheck** all boxes here (Telemetry, Crash Reports, etc.) to stop Firefox from
  sending usage data back to Mozilla.
- **HTTPS-Only Mode:** Enable **"HTTPS-Only Mode in all windows"** to ensure your connections to websites are always
  encrypted.
- **Query Parameter Stripping:** Set this to **Strict** (or ensure it's on) to strip tracking identifiers (like
  `?utm_source=...`) from URLs when you click links.

## Step 2: Advanced Tweaks via `about:config`

To access Firefox's hidden database of advanced settings, type `about:config` into your address bar, press Enter, and accept
the warning.

Use the search bar at the top to locate the following preferences, and double-click them to toggle their values:

### 1. Enable Tor-style Fingerprinting Resistance

This activates built-in protections taken from the Tor Browser, which spoof your timezone to UTC, standardize your user
agent, and restrict canvas tracking.

- `privacy.resistFingerprinting` ➔ Set to **`true`**

> _Note: This will force your browser to use a light theme on some sites and may show gray borders around pages
> (letterboxing) to hide your exact monitor size._

### 2. Isolate First-Party Cookies

This forces cookies to stay isolated within the specific website you are visiting, preventing them from being used to track
you across different websites.

- `privacy.firstparty.isolate` ➔ Set to **`true`**

### 3. Prevent WebRTC IP Leaks

WebRTC is used for voice/video chat in the browser, but it can leak your real, physical IP address—even if you are using a
VPN.

- `media.peerconnection.enabled` ➔ Set to **`false`**

> _Warning: This will break web-based video calling tools like Google Meet or Discord in the browser. Toggle it back to
> `true` if you need them._

### 4. Disable Pocket and Sponsored Content

This removes Mozilla's built-in feed integrations and sponsored articles on your blank new tab page.

- `extensions.pocket.enabled` ➔ Set to **`false`**
- `browser.newtabpage.activity-stream.showSponsored` ➔ Set to **`false`**
- `browser.newtabpage.activity-stream.showSponsoredTopSites` ➔ Set to **`false`**

### 5. Stop Speculative Connections

By default, Firefox will quietly pre-load links it predicts you might click on. This leaks your IP to sites you haven't even
visited yet.

- `browser.urlbar.speculativeConnect.enabled` ➔ Set to **`false`**

## Step 3: Add Essential Extensions

You do not need a dozen browser extensions to stay safe; in fact, having too many makes your browser fingerprint more unique.
Stick to these two powerhouses:

1. **uBlock Origin:** The absolute gold standard of ad and content blockers. It is incredibly lightweight and highly
   customizable.
2. **Firefox Multi-Account Containers:** An official extension by Mozilla that lets you isolate your different online
   identities (e.g., Work, Shopping, Personal) into separate color-coded tabs so websites can't link your browsing habits
   together.

---

## The Automated Way: "Arkenfox" & "LibreWolf"

If you don't want to manually toggle dozens of settings, you have two excellent, community-vetted alternatives:

- **Arkenfox user.js:** This is an open-source, highly maintained template configuration file. You drop the `user.js` file
  into your Firefox profile folder, and it automatically applies hundreds of hardened privacy and security settings.
- **LibreWolf:** If you want a browser that has already done all of this work for you, download LibreWolf. It is a custom
  fork of Firefox that comes completely stripped of telemetry, has uBlock Origin pre-installed, and has advanced hardening
  settings turned on by default.
