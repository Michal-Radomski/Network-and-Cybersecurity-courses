While both extensions are designed to block ads, **uBlock Origin** and **Adblock Plus** are fundamentally different under the
hood. The main differences come down to performance, philosophy, and corporate backings.

Here is how they stack up against each other:

---

## 1. Resource Consumption (Performance)

The biggest difference you will notice on your device is speed.

- **uBlock Origin:** Built from the ground up to be lightweight. It uses incredibly low CPU and RAM. It blocks ads before
  they even download, which speeds up webpage load times and extends battery life on laptops.
- **Adblock Plus:** Known to be a resource hog. It consumes significantly more computer memory (RAM) and can noticeably slow
  down your browser, especially if you have many tabs open.

## 2. The "Acceptable Ads" Program (Philosophy)

This is where the two extensions differ ethically.

- **Adblock Plus:** Runs a controversial program called **"Acceptable Ads."** By default, they allow certain "non-intrusive"
  ads to bypass their filter. Massive advertising companies (including Google) pay Adblock Plus a massive fee to be
  whitelisted. While you can turn this feature off in the settings, many users dislike that a tool meant to block ads profits
  from letting them through.
- **uBlock Origin:** Completely independent and strictly open-source. The creator, Raymond Hill, refuses to take donations,
  corporate sponsorships, or participate in any whitelisting programs. If an ad tracker exists, it gets blocked. Period.

## 3. Scope of Protection

- **Adblock Plus:** Primarily focuses on blocking standard cosmetic ads (like banners and video pop-ups).
- **uBlock Origin:** Is not just an ad blocker; it is a **wide-spectrum content blocker**. Out of the box, it stops ads,
  third-party trackers, coin-miners, malicious URLs, and annoying cookie pop-up notices. It also gives users advanced tools
  to block specific elements on a webpage manually.
