When looking at who collects the most private data, companies generally fall into two categories: **Big Tech Giants** (who
you actively give data to) and **Data Brokers** (who buy and sell your data invisibly).

Independent privacy audits and reports from regulators like the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) show the biggest offenders in
each category.

---

## 1. The Big Tech Giants (The "Front-Facing" Spies)

These companies monitor you across multiple devices, apps, and websites to build massive advertising profiles.

### Google (Alphabet)

Google is widely rated by privacy researchers as the company that collects the most data. Because their services are deeply
woven into daily life, they track:

- Every search query and clicked link.
- Every video watched on **YouTube**.
- Precise location tracking through **Google Maps** (even when you aren't using the app).
- Every email received or sent in **Gmail**.
- Browsing habits on the millions of external websites that use **Google Analytics** or Google ad networks.

### Meta (Facebook, Instagram, Threads, WhatsApp)

Meta's data collection is incredibly invasive because it focuses on your psychology, relationships, and behavior.

- They track your personal messages, photos, and video interactions.
- They log metadata from your photos (exactly where and when they were taken).
- Through the invisible **Meta Pixel** tracker embedded on millions of non-Meta shopping, news, and health websites, they
  track what you look at across the web even if you don't have a Facebook account.

### TikTok (ByteDance)

TikTok is highly aggressive with device-level data harvesting. Security audits show the app aggressively logs device IDs,
contact lists, exact locations, and typing rhythms (keystroke dynamics). It also utilizes an in-app browser that can
theoretically track everything you type while inside the app.

### Amazon

Amazon tracks your digital and physical life. They log every item you click, search for, or buy, building a massive profile
of your income level and lifestyle. Furthermore, through smart home ecosystems like **Ring doorbells**, **Alexa speakers**,
and **Blink cameras**, they gather data on your physical routines and home environment.

---

## 2. The Data Brokers (The "Invisible" Spies)

These companies do not have consumer-facing apps. Instead, they operate in the shadows, buying up data from credit card
companies, public records, DMV registries, voter files, and app developers to stitch together terrifyingly detailed dossiers
on hundreds of millions of people.

| Company                           | What They Are Known For                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Acxiom**                        | One of the largest data brokers in the world. They hold profiles on over 2.5 billion people, tracking details down to your estimated income, the car you drive, and your exact buying habits.                 |
| **Experian, Equifax, TransUnion** | While known as credit bureaus, they run massive data brokerage wings. They track your financial health, loans, debts, payment histories, and employment changes.                                              |
| **LexisNexis**                    | Specializes in public records and legal data. They aggregate court filings, property deeds, phone registries, and address histories, frequently selling this data to insurance companies and law enforcement. |
| **Oracle**                        | Though primarily an enterprise tech company, Oracle operates a massive cloud data marketplace that tracks billions of consumer profiles, mapping offline purchases to online behavior for advertisers.        |

---

## 3. Data Intensive Apps: A Quick Comparison

According to privacy research from firms like _Surfshark_, certain everyday commercial apps collect a disproportionate amount
of data linked directly to your identity:

- **Social Media:** Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok link **100%** of available user data points (browsing history, contact
  info, identifiers) to your identity.
- **Food Delivery & Shopping:** **Wish**, **DoorDash**, and **Uber Eats** are frequently flagged as the most data-intensive
  apps in their categories, tracking and sharing roughly 40% of the data points they collect (like your precise location and
  phone number) with external advertising networks.
