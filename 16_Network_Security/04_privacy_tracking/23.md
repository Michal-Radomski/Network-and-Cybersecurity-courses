Reducing your browser fingerprint is all about **blending into the crowd**. Instead of trying to hide your data, the goal is
to make your browser look exactly like thousands of other users. If your browser looks identical to everyone else's, trackers
cannot tell you apart.

Here is how you can effectively reduce your browser fingerprint on both Ubuntu and Windows.

---

## 1. Use the Right Browser

The software you use to surf the web is the most critical factor. Standard browsers like Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge are
highly susceptible to fingerprinting. Instead, choose one of the following:

### Option A: Mullvad Browser or Tor Browser (Maximum Protection)

Both of these browsers are specifically designed to combat fingerprinting. They do this by making **all users look exactly
the same**.

- **Tor Browser:** Routes your traffic through the Tor network for absolute anonymity.
- **Mullvad Browser:** Co-developed with the Tor Project, it has the exact same anti-fingerprinting defenses as Tor but is
  designed to be used with a standard internet connection or VPN (without the slower speeds of the Tor network).
- _Available on both Ubuntu and Windows._

### Option B: LibreWolf or Firefox (High Protection)

If you want a normal daily-driver browser, **LibreWolf** is a privacy-focused fork of Firefox that has anti-fingerprinting
features enabled by default.

If you prefer standard **Firefox**, you can manually enable these protections:

1. Open Firefox and type `about:config` in the address bar.
2. Search for `privacy.resistFingerprinting`.
3. Double-click it to change the value to **true**.

### Option C: Brave Browser (Balanced Protection)

Instead of trying to make you look like everyone else, Brave uses a technique called **farbling**. It subtly randomizes the
data your browser sends to websites (like Canvas and WebGL details) every time a site asks for it. This makes your
fingerprint look different on every single website you visit, preventing cross-site tracking.

---

## 2. Browser Hygiene (Both OS platforms)

Changing your browser is only half the battle. Your habits also affect your fingerprint. Follow these best practices:

- **Do not maximize your browser window:** If you use Mullvad, Tor, or Firefox with `resistFingerprinting` enabled, you will
  notice gray bars around the webpage. This is called **letterboxing**. It keeps your inner browser window at a standard size
  so websites cannot detect your physical monitor's exact screen resolution.
- **Minimize extensions:** Every extension you install can be detected by websites. Keep your extensions to a bare minimum
  (ideally just a trusted ad blocker like uBlock Origin, or none at all if using Mullvad/Tor).
- **Keep your browser in English:** Websites often check your browser’s preferred language. Using a rare language makes you
  stand out; sticking to English helps you blend in.

---

## 3. OS-Specific Adjustments

While fingerprinting mostly happens inside the browser, some operating system settings bleed through.

### On Ubuntu:

- **Stick to default system fonts:** Ubuntu comes with a very standard set of fonts. Avoid installing custom font packages
  (like Microsoft Core Fonts) unless you absolutely need them, as a unique list of system fonts is a primary way websites
  identify you.
- **Install browsers via Flatpak or Official Tarballs:** If you install browsers like Tor or Mullvad, using the Flatpak
  versions (via the Software Center) sandbox the application, preventing it from seeing local system files and settings that
  could be used for fingerprinting.

### On Windows:

- **Limit font installations:** Similar to Ubuntu, do not install massive custom font packs (like those used for graphic
  design) on your primary web-browsing machine.
- **Turn down OS telemetry:** Windows naturally sends a lot of diagnostic data. Turn off unnecessary diagnostic data in
  **Settings > Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback** to prevent OS-level identifiers from syncing with browser-based
  tracking.

### Comparison of Anti-Fingerprinting Approaches

| Browser                 | Anti-Fingerprinting Strategy                                                          | Ease of Use for Daily Tasks                      |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Tor / Mullvad**       | **Spoofing & Standardization** (Forces everyone to look identical; uses letterboxing) | Moderate (Some sites might prompt more CAPTCHAs) |
| **Brave**               | **Farbling** (Randomizes hardware data on every site)                                 | High (Acts like a normal, fast browser)          |
| **LibreWolf / Firefox** | **Resist Fingerprinting (RFP)** (Standardizes time zones, hides system info)          | High/Moderate                                    |
