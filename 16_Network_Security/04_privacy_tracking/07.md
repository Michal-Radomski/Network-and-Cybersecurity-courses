When it comes to tracking, search engines generally fall into two extremes: the "Data Giants" that track almost everything to
build a profile on you, and the "Privacy Guards" that intentionally strip away your personal data.

---

## The Ones That Track You the Most

These search engines are built on a "surveillance capitalism" model. They provide excellent, highly personalized results, but
they do so by collecting massive amounts of data about your identity and habits.

### 1. Google (The Biggest Tracker)

Google tracks the most data by far, primarily because it doesn't just look at your search history. It connects your searches
to a massive web of your other activities:

- Your exact location history via **Google Maps**.
- Every video you watch or comment on via **YouTube**.
- The contents of your **Gmail** inbox and **Google Drive**.
- Your browsing habits on millions of non-Google websites that use **Google Analytics** or Google ads.

### 2. Microsoft Bing

Bing functions very similarly to Google. It tracks your search history, device information, and location, especially if you
are logged into a Windows or Microsoft account. This data is fed into Microsoft's advertising network and used to train its
integrated AI features (like Copilot).

### 3. Yahoo! Search

Yahoo is owned by a massive media and advertising conglomerate. It tracks your search terms, location, and reading habits
across Yahoo Finance, News, and Sports to serve highly targeted advertising.

---

## The Ones That Track You the Least (Or Not At All)

These private alternatives do not build a profile on you, do not log your IP address, and do not track you across the web.
Instead of targeted ads based on your identity, they show "contextual ads" (e.g., if you search for "bicycles," you see a
bicycle ad, but the engine instantly forgets you ever searched for it).

### 1. Brave Search (Highly Recommended)

Brave Search is completely private and does not track your queries, IP address, or digital fingerprint. Crucially, it uses
its own completely independent index to find web pages, meaning it doesn't rely on Google or Microsoft to fetch your results.

### 2. DuckDuckGo

The most famous privacy search engine. DuckDuckGo does not store your search history or personal information. It pulls most
of its search results from Bing, but it acts as a protective shield—Microsoft never sees _who_ is making the search.

### 3. Startpage

If you love Google’s search accuracy but hate its tracking, Startpage is the middle ground. It pays Google to use its search
algorithm. When you type a query, Startpage strips out all your personal data (like your IP address and location) and submits
it to Google anonymously. You get Google-quality results with zero tracking.

### 4. Mojeek

Mojeek is a strictly no-tracking search engine based in the UK. What makes it unique is that it is 100% independent—it uses
its own web crawlers to build its own index of the internet, completely free from the influence of Big Tech.
