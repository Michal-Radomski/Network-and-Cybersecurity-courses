To secure a laptop running both Windows and Ubuntu (a dual-boot setup) against physical theft, the absolute most important
step is **Full Disk Encryption (FDE)**.

If a laptop is stolen, an attacker can easily bypass standard login passwords by removing the hard drive or booting into a
live USB to read your files. Encryption prevents this by turning your data into unreadable gibberish without your password.

Here is how to secure both operating systems, secure the hardware, and prepare for a theft scenario.

---

## 1. Encrypt Both Operating Systems

Because you are dual-booting, you must ensure that **both** partitions are fully encrypted. If you only encrypt Windows, a
thief can boot into Ubuntu and access any shared unencrypted data (or vice versa).

### For Windows: Turn on BitLocker

Windows Pro, Enterprise, and Education editions come with BitLocker. (If you have Windows Home, look for "Device Encryption"
in your settings).

1. Open the Start menu, type **Manage BitLocker**, and select it.
2. Click **Turn on BitLocker** for your Windows drive (usually `C:`).
3. Choose how you want to unlock your drive at startup (using your computer's built-in **TPM chip** is highly recommended, as
   it binds the encryption to your specific motherboard).
4. **Crucial:** Save your **BitLocker Recovery Key** in a secure place _outside_ of this laptop (like your Microsoft cloud
   account or printed out at home). If your hardware fails or glitches, you will need this key to get your data back.

### For Ubuntu: Use LUKS Encryption

The easiest and most secure way to encrypt Ubuntu is to enable it **during the installation process**.

- **If you are installing fresh:** When choosing the installation type, select **Advanced features** and choose **Encrypt the
  new Ubuntu installation for security** (this uses LUKS, Linux Unified Key Setup). You will choose a security security
  passphrase that you must type every time the computer boots up.
- **If Ubuntu is already installed without encryption:** Encrypting an existing Linux partition without wiping it is
  incredibly complex and risky. The safest route is to back up your personal files, reinstall Ubuntu, and check the
  encryption box during setup.

---

## 2. Secure the Hardware (UEFI/BIOS Lock)

Even with encryption, a thief could boot into a USB flash drive to wipe your computer and resell it, or try to tamper with
your boot settings. You need to lock down the motherboard.

1. Shut down your laptop and turn it on while pressing your manufacturer's BIOS/UEFI key (usually `F2`, `F12`, `Del`, or
   `Esc`).
2. Set a **Supervisor/Administrator Password** for the BIOS. This prevents anyone from changing system settings without
   authorization.
3. **Disable Booting from USB/External Devices** or change the boot order so the internal hard drive is always first.
4. Ensure **Secure Boot** is enabled. This ensures that only trusted operating systems (like your verified Windows and Ubuntu
   installations) are allowed to load.

---

## 3. Secure the Lock Screens

If your laptop is stolen while it is turned on, the encryption won't help if your lock screen is easy to bypass or doesn't
activate quickly.

- **Short Timeout:** Set both Windows and Ubuntu to lock the screen after **1 to 2 minutes** of inactivity.
- **Strong User Passwords:** Ensure your local account passwords for both OSes are strong and not easily guessable.
- **Disable Lock Screen Notifications:** Turn off sensitive notification previews (like emails or texts) on the Windows and
  Ubuntu lock screens so a thief can't read your private data while the device is locked.

---

## 4. Setting up "Find My Device" (Windows Only)

While Linux doesn't have a native, built-in tracking network, Windows does.

1. On Windows, go to **Settings > Privacy & security > Find my device**.
2. Turn the toggle to **On**.
3. If the laptop is stolen and the thief connects it to Wi-Fi (even on the lock screen), you can log into your Microsoft
   account from another device to see its last known location or lock it remotely.

---

## Summary Checklist

| Action Item                  | Why it Matters                                                                    |
| ---------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **BitLocker (Windows)**      | Keeps Windows files completely unreadable if the drive is pulled out.             |
| **LUKS Encryption (Ubuntu)** | Protects your Linux files from being read via a live USB or drive extraction.     |
| **BIOS/UEFI Password**       | Stops a thief from booting into a USB stick to bypass security or wipe the drive. |
| **Aggressive Sleep Timer**   | Locks the laptop quickly if you walk away from it or if it's grabbed while open.  |
