A **YubiKey** is a small, hardware-based security device made by a company called Yubico. Think of it like a physical key for
your digital life. Instead of relying only on a password or a text message code to log into your accounts, you plug this
little device into your phone or computer (or tap it using NFC) to prove you are actually you.

It is widely considered one of the most secure ways to protect your emails, social media, and school accounts from hackers.

---

## How It Works

When you log into a website that supports hardware keys (like Google, Discord, or Microsoft), the process looks like this:

1. **Enter your password** as you normally would.
2. **Plug in or tap your YubiKey** when prompted.
3. **Touch the gold circle** on the key. The key uses advanced cryptography to instantly verify your identity and unlock your
   account.

---

## Key Features

- **Phishing Protection:** Even if a hacker tricks you into giving them your password on a fake website, they can't log in
  because they don't have your physical YubiKey.
- **No Battery Required:** It doesn't need to be charged. It draws a tiny bit of power directly from your phone or computer
  when you connect it.
- **Durable and Waterproof:** They are built to live on a keychain and can survive being dropped, stepped on, or even put
  through the washing machine.
- **Multiple Connections:** YubiKeys come in different shapes and sizes, including **USB-A, USB-C, and Lightning**, and most
  have **NFC** so you can just tap them against the back of your phone.

---

## Why Use One?

Standard Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)—like getting a code texted to your phone—is good, but hackers can sometimes
intercept text messages or trick you into revealing the code. Because a YubiKey requires you to physically touch the device
attached to your computer or phone, it makes it incredibly difficult for someone on the other side of the world to hack your
account.

> **Tip:** If you decide to use a YubiKey for your important accounts, it is highly recommended to have a **backup key**
> registered to your accounts as well, just in case you lose your primary one!
