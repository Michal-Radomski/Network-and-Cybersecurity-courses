Setting up a home router securely is one of the best ways to protect your personal data and devices from cyber threats.

Here is a step-by-step guide to locking down your home network.

---

## 1. Access Your Router's Admin Panel

To change your security settings, you need to log into your router’s backend.

- **Find the IP address:** It’s usually printed on a sticker on the bottom of the router (commonly `192.168.0.1` or
  `192.168.1.1`).
- **Log in:** Open a web browser, type that IP address into the URL bar, and press Enter.

## 2. Change the Default Credentials

Most routers come with universal default logins (like username: `admin`, password: `password`). Hackers know these by heart.

- **Change the Admin Password:** Create a strong, unique password for the router's management settings.
- _Note: This is different from the Wi-Fi password your devices use to connect to the internet._

## 3. Update the Wi-Fi Encryption and Password

Your wireless traffic needs to be scrambled so neighbors or attackers can't intercept your data.

- **Select the strongest security protocol:** \* **WPA3:** Use this if your router and devices support it (it’s the most
  secure).
- **WPA2 (AES):** Use this if WPA3 isn't available. Avoid WPA, WEP, or "TKIP" as they are outdated and easily cracked.

- **Create a strong Wi-Fi passphrase:** Use a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

## 4. Change the Network Name (SSID)

The default SSID often broadcasts the make and model of your router (e.g., `NETGEAR_5G`), telling hackers exactly what
vulnerabilities to look for.

- Change it to something generic that doesn't identify you, your family, or your router brand.

## 5. Enable a Guest Network

If your router supports it, turn on a separate **Guest Network** for visitors and smart home (IoT) devices.

- **Why?** Smart TVs, smart bulbs, and cheap IoT devices often have weak security. If a hacker breaches a smart bulb on your
  guest network, they won't be able to access your main computer or phone.

## 6. Disable Risky Features

Turn off features that make your router vulnerable to automated attacks:

- **Disable WPS (Wi-Fi Protected Setup):** While convenient for pairing devices with a button, WPS has known security flaws
  that allow hackers to brute-force their way into your network.
- **Disable Remote Management:** Ensure you can only log into your router's settings while physically connected to your home
  network, not from anywhere on the internet.
- **Disable UPnP (Universal Plug and Play):** This allows devices to open ports automatically. While helpful for gaming,
  malware can abuse it to open backdoors.

## 7. Keep the Firmware Updated

Routers are basically small computers, and their software (firmware) gets security patches just like your phone does.

- Check the router settings for an **Auto-Update** feature and turn it on.
- If it doesn't support auto-updates, log in every few months to check for and install updates manually.

---

### Quick Checklist

| Feature                 | Recommended Setting                         |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **Admin Password**      | Changed from default to a strong passphrase |
| **Wi-Fi Security Type** | WPA3 or WPA2 (AES)                          |
| **WPS & UPnP**          | Disabled                                    |
| **Remote Management**   | Disabled                                    |
| **Guest Network**       | Enabled (for guests and IoT devices)        |
| **Firmware**            | Set to Auto-Update                          |
