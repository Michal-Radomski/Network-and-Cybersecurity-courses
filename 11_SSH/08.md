When you generate an SSH key pair using `ssh-keygen`, you will often see an ASCII art graphic printed in your terminal. This
is called **randomart** (or the **Visual Host Key**).

---

## 🎨 What is Randomart?

Randomart is a visual representation of your SSH key's cryptographic fingerprint.

Because standard cryptographic fingerprints are long, abstract strings of hexadecimal numbers (e.g., `SHA256:hD7x8...`), they
are incredibly difficult for humans to memorize or quickly compare. Randomart converts that abstract string into a structured
$9 \times 9$ grid of symbols that your brain can recognize as a pattern.

---

## 🧠 Why is it used?

The primary reason for randomart is **human pattern recognition**.

Humans are notoriously bad at noticing if a single character changes in a long string of random text, but we are excellent at
noticing when a visual pattern changes.

- **The "Man-in-the-Middle" Check:** If you frequently connect to a server, you might subconsciously memorize the shape of
  its randomart. If an attacker intercepts your connection and presents a fake key, the randomart will look completely
  different, instantly alerting you to the change.

---

## 🧱 How It's Generated (The "Drunkard's Walk")

The algorithm used to create the image is called the **Drunkard's Walk**:

1. The algorithm starts a "walker" exactly in the center of a $9 \times 9$ grid.
2. It takes the SSH key's fingerprint and breaks it down into pairs of bits.
3. Each pair of bits acts as a direction, telling the walker to move one step diagonally (Up-Left, Up-Right, Down-Left, or
   Down-Right).
4. Every time the walker lands on a square, the "coin" or counter for that square increases.
5. Once the walk is finished, the final graphic replaces the numbers in each square with specific symbols based on how many
   times the walker visited them (e.g., `.`, `o`, `+`, `*`, `B`, `X`).

### Key Symbols to Note:

- **`S`**: The **Start** position (always in the center).
- **`E`**: The **End** position (where the walker finished its route).

---

## 🔍 Example Breakdown

Here is what a typical randomart output looks like:

```text
+--[ED25519 256]--+
|       . . . .   |
|      o . o o    |
|     o . o o .   |
|    . . S . +    |
|     . o B * .   |
|    o = * X o    |
|   . = o = o     |
|    . .   o      |
|         .E      |
+----[SHA256]-----+

```

If you were to generate a completely different key, the path of the "drunkard" would be entirely different, resulting in a
completely distinct visual shape.

> 💡 **Tip:** You can see the randomart of any existing local key at any time by running:
>
> ```bash
> ssh-keygen -lv -f ~/.ssh/id_ed25519
>
> ```
