Adding an intermediate TLS certificate to your Apache configuration depends slightly on which version of Apache you are
running. Modern versions (Apache 2.4.8 and later) have simplified this process.

Here is how to do it.

---

## Step 1: Prepare Your Certificate Files

Ensure you have the following three files ready on your server:

1. **Your Domain Certificate:** (e.g., `your_domain.crt`)
2. **The Intermediate Certificate:** (e.g., `intermediate.crt`)
3. **Your Private Key:** (e.g., `your_domain.key`)

---

## Step 2: Configure Apache

Choose the method below that matches your Apache version. (You can check your version by running `apache2 -v` or `httpd -v`).

### Method A: Apache 2.4.8 and Newer (Recommended)

In newer versions, you do not use a separate directive for the intermediate certificate. Instead, you **combine** your domain
certificate and the intermediate certificate into a single file.

1. **Combine the certificates:** Open a terminal and concatenate the files. **Crucial:** Your domain certificate must come
   first, followed immediately by the intermediate certificate.

```bash
cat your_domain.crt intermediate.crt > fullchain.crt

```

2. **Update your Apache configuration file** (usually located in `/etc/apache2/sites-available/` or `/etc/httpd/conf.d/`):

```apache
<VirtualHost *:443>
    ServerName yourdomain.com
    DocumentRoot /var/www/html

    SSLEngine on
    SSLCertificateFile /path/to/fullchain.crt
    SSLCertificateKeyFile /path/to/your_domain.key
</VirtualHost>

```

### Method B: Apache 2.4.7 and Older

If you are running an older legacy version, you will keep the files separate and use the `SSLCertificateChainFile` directive.

1. **Update your Apache configuration file:**

```apache
<VirtualHost *:443>
    ServerName yourdomain.com
    DocumentRoot /var/www/html

    SSLEngine on
    SSLCertificateFile /path/to/your_domain.crt
    SSLCertificateChainFile /path/to/intermediate.crt
    SSLCertificateKeyFile /path/to/your_domain.key
</VirtualHost>

```

---

## Step 3: Test and Restart Apache

Before restarting, always test your configuration to ensure there are no syntax errors that could crash your web server.

1. **Test the configuration:**

- On Ubuntu/Debian: `sudo apache2ctl configtest`
- On CentOS/RHEL: `sudo httpd -t`

2. **If it says `Syntax OK`, restart Apache:**

- On Ubuntu/Debian: `sudo systemctl restart apache2`
- On CentOS/RHEL: `sudo systemctl restart httpd`

> **Tip:** After restarting, you can verify that your intermediate certificate is being sent correctly by using an online
> tool like the SSL Labs Server Test.
