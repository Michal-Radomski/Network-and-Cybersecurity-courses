To understand what a 2048-bit number looks like in the decimal system (the base-10 numbers we use every day), we have to look
at the maximum value that many bits can hold.

In the binary system, a 2048-bit number is a string of 2,048 ones and zeros.

---

### How Big is This Number, Really?

To give you some perspective on just how unfathomably huge a 617-digit number is:

- **The Number of Atoms in the Observable Universe:** Scientists estimate there are about $10^{80}$ atoms in the entire
  universe. That is an 81-digit number.
- **A 2048-bit Number:** This number is roughly $10^{616}$.

This means a 2048-bit number is vastly larger than the total number of atoms in the universe. In fact, you could imagine a
universe inside every atom, and a universe inside those atoms, for several layers deep, and you still wouldn't come close to
a number this size.

### Why Do We Use It?

This sheer size is exactly why 2048-bit encryption (like the RSA keys used in the SSL/TLS certificates mentioned earlier) is
so secure. Trying to guess a specific 2048-bit private key by pure luck or brute-force guessing is mathematically impossible
with current computers, because there are simply too many possibilities to count.
