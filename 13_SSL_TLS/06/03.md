A Certificate Signing Request (CSR) is an encrypted block of text that you send to a Certificate Authority (like Let's
Encrypt, DigiCert, or Namecheap) to apply for an SSL/TLS certificate. It contains your public key and information about your
organization.

Here is how to generate a CSR using OpenSSL.

---

### The Standard Command (Generates CSR + New Private Key)

The most common way to do this is to generate a brand new private key and the CSR at the exact same time. Run this command in
your terminal:

```bash
openssl req -new -newkey rsa:2048 -nodes -keyout yourdomain.key -out yourdomain.csr

```

#### What this command means:

- **`req`**: Activates the OpenSSL utility for certificate requests.
- **`-new`**: Indicates you want to generate a _new_ request.
- **`-newkey rsa:2048`**: Creates a secure 2048-bit RSA private key along with it.
- **`-nodes`**: (No DES) Ensures the private key isn't locked with a password, allowing your web server to restart
  automatically without human intervention.
- **`-keyout`**: The filename for your private key (keep this a secret).
- **`-out`**: The filename for your actual CSR (this is what you give to the provider).

---

### Step 2: Fill Out the Information

Once you press Enter, OpenSSL will prompt you for details about your website and organization. Fill them out as follows:

- **Country Name (2 letter code):** Your country's two-letter abbreviation (e.g., `US`, `CA`, `GB`).
- **State or Province Name:** The full name of your state or province (e.g., `California`). Do not use abbreviations.
- **Locality Name:** Your city (e.g., `San Francisco`).
- **Organization Name:** Your legal company name (e.g., `My Awesome Company LLC`). If you don't have a company, you can just
  use your name or your website's name.
- **Organizational Unit Name:** Your department (e.g., `IT` or `Web Security`). You can also press Enter to leave this blank.
- **Common Name:** **This is the most critical field.** Type the exact domain name you want to secure (e.g., `yourdomain.com`
  or `www.yourdomain.com`).
- _Note: If you are ordering a Wildcard certificate, use an asterisk like this: `_.yourdomain.com`.\*

- **Email Address:** Your administrative email address.
- **A challenge password / Optional company name:** **Leave these blank.** Just press Enter to skip them, as modern
  Certificate Authorities do not use them and they can cause issues.

---

### What to do with the files?

After finishing the prompts, you will have two new files in your directory:

1. **`yourdomain.key`**: This is your Private Key. **Never share this file with anyone.** Leave it safely on your server.
2. **`yourdomain.csr`**: This is your Certificate Signing Request.

Open the `.csr` file with a text editor (like `cat yourdomain.csr` or using Notepad/Nano). It will look like a big block of
scrambled text starting with `-----BEGIN CERTIFICATE REQUEST-----`.

Copy that entire block of text—including the BEGIN and END lines—and paste it into your Certificate Authority’s website
portal to order your SSL certificate.
