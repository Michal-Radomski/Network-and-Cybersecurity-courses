Setting up Cloudflare as a reverse proxy for your website is a great way to improve performance, get a free SSL certificate,
and protect your site from attacks.

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting it configured:

### Step 1: Create a Cloudflare Account and Add Your Site

1. Go to [Cloudflare's website](https://www.cloudflare.com/) and sign up for a free account.
2. Once logged in, click **Add a Site**.
3. Enter your domain name (e.g., `example.com`) and click **Add Site**.
4. Select the **Free plan** (which includes the reverse proxy, CDN, and basic DDoS protection) and click **Continue**.

---

### Step 2: Review Your DNS Records

Cloudflare will automatically scan your domain's existing DNS records.

1. Review the list to make sure your main website records (like your `A`, `AAAA`, or `CNAME` records pointing to your web
   host) are present.
2. Look at the **Proxy status** column. To enable Cloudflare as a reverse proxy, the toggle next to your main domain records
   must be set to **Proxied** (indicated by an **orange cloud** icon).

- _Note: If it is set to "DNS only" (grey cloud), traffic will go straight to your server, bypassing Cloudflare's proxy._

3. Click **Continue**.

---

### Step 3: Update Your Nameservers at Your Registrar

To route your traffic through Cloudflare, you need to point your domain's nameservers to Cloudflare.

1. Cloudflare will provide you with two new nameservers (e.g., `ashley.ns.cloudflare.com` and `oliver.ns.cloudflare.com`).
2. Log in to the registrar where you bought your domain (like Namecheap, GoDaddy, Google Domains, etc.).
3. Find the **DNS Management** or **Nameservers** settings for your domain.
4. Change the nameservers from your current provider's to the specific ones provided by Cloudflare.
5. Save your changes.

> ⚠️ **Note:** DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 24 hours to propagate worldwide, though it usually happens
> quite fast.

---

### Step 4: Configure Encryption (SSL/TLS)

Once Cloudflare is active, you want to ensure traffic between your users, Cloudflare, and your origin server is secure.

1. In your Cloudflare dashboard, go to the **SSL/TLS** tab.
2. Choose your encryption mode:

- **Flexible:** Encrypts traffic between the user and Cloudflare, but traffic from Cloudflare to your server is unencrypted.
  (Easiest to set up if your server doesn't have an SSL certificate).
- **Full or Full (Strict):** Encrypts the entire path. _Highly recommended_, but requires you to have an SSL certificate
  installed on your origin server (Cloudflare provides free Origin Certificates you can install for this).

---

### How to Verify It's Working

Once the nameservers update, you can check if the reverse proxy is active:

- **Check the dashboard:** Your Cloudflare home screen will say "Great news! Cloudflare is now protecting your site."
- **Check the Network Headers:** Open your website, right-click and choose **Inspect**, go to the **Network** tab, refresh
  the page, and click on your domain. Under **Response Headers**, you should see `server: cloudflare`.
