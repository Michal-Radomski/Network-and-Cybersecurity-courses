The organizations that issue root certificates are divided into three primary categories: **Automated/Free CAs** (which
handle the vast majority of daily internet traffic), **Traditional Commercial CAs** (which focus heavily on enterprise and
custom security solutions), and **Tech Platform CAs** (built directly into ecosystems).

---

### 1. Automated & Free Certificate Authorities

These organizations use automated protocols (like ACME) to issue short-lived, domain-validated certificates entirely for
free. They have completely transformed web security over the last decade.

- **Let's Encrypt (ISRG):** By far the largest Root CA in the world, issuing more than 50% of all active certificates on the
  web. It is a non-profit backed by major tech firms.
- **Google Trust Services (GTS):** Google's own CA infrastructure. It handles a massive share of the web, frequently used to
  secure Google's own properties as well as websites using Cloudflare and Google Cloud.
- **Amazon Trust Services (ATS):** Amazon's built-in root authority, which automatically issues and manages free SSL/TLS
  certificates for infrastructure running inside Amazon Web Services (AWS).

### 2. Major Commercial & Enterprise CAs

These are traditional, paid, or enterprise-focused CAs. They issue standard web certificates but also specialize in
organizational validation, code signing, and complex Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) management.

- **Sectigo (formerly Comodo):** One of the largest commercial CAs in existence. They own several sub-brands (like USERTrust)
  and issue a huge volume of retail and enterprise certificates.
- **DigiCert:** The industry standard for high-assurance enterprise security, financial institutions, and government bodies.
  DigiCert acquired Symantec's legacy certificate business and also runs brands like GeoTrust and Thawte.
- **GlobalSign:** A highly trusted international commercial CA focusing heavily on enterprise identity management, IoT device
  security, and cloud integrations.
- **GoDaddy:** While primarily known as a domain registrar, GoDaddy runs its own highly trusted Root CA to provide built-in
  security for the domains hosted on its platform.
- **Entrust:** A legacy player in data protection and identity management that operates a trusted public root infrastructure.

### 3. Operating System & Platform CAs

Major tech companies maintain their own root certificate authorities strictly to manage trust and identity within their own
ecosystems, hardware, and operating system updates.

- **Microsoft Root Authority**
- **Apple Root CA**

---

### How to See Them Live

If you want to see the exact ones your own machine relies on right now, you can open your OS trust store. For example, on
Windows, hitting `Win + R` and typing `certmgr.msc` will let you browse the exact list of trusted roots hardcoded into your
system.
