A good password focuses on **length** and **uniqueness** rather than forced, complicated character rules.

Modern cybersecurity standards (like the NIST digital identity guidelines) have shifted away from arbitrary rules like
changing passwords every month or adding random exclamation points.

---

## The 3 Rules of a Great Password

### 1. Make It Long (Use Passphrases)

Length is the most critical factor in stopping automated cracking tools. Aim for **at least 15 characters**.

The easiest way to make a long, readable password is to combine 4 or 5 completely unrelated, random words into a
**passphrase**.

- ❌ **Bad:** `P@ssword1!` _(Short, predictable pattern)_
- ❌ **Bad:** `Tr0ub4dor&3` _(Hard to remember, easy for machines to guess)_
- ✅ **Good:** `coffee-table-planet-violin-yellow` _(Long, super secure, easy to type)_

### 2. Make It Unique

Never reuse the same password across multiple accounts. If a site suffers a data leak, attackers will immediately try that
stolen password on every major service (email, banking, social media).

### 3. Use a Password Manager

Human memory isn't built to store dozens of 16-character passwords. Use a reputable password manager (like Bitwarden,
1Password, or Proton Pass) to generate and store randomized credentials for every service you use.

---

## Quick Checklist

| Practice                      | Recommended?    | Why?                                                                         |
| ----------------------------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Passphrases (15+ chars)**   | ✅ **Yes**      | Massive search space for hackers to brute-force.                             |
| **Password Manager**          | ✅ **Yes**      | Prevents password reuse across accounts.                                     |
| **Multi-Factor Auth (MFA)**   | ✅ **Yes**      | Stops attackers even if they somehow discover your password.                 |
| **Random special characters** | ⚠️ **Optional** | Extra length is much more effective than swapping an `a` for `@`.            |
| **Changing every 90 days**    | ❌ **No**       | Forces people to make slight, predictable edits (e.g., `Spring2026!`).       |
| **Personal Info / Names**     | ❌ **No**       | Birthdays, pet names, and user handles are easily scraped from social media. |
