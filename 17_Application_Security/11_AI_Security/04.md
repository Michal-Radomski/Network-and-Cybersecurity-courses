Navigating AI safely comes down to two main goals: **protecting your personal data** and **critically evaluating what the AI
gives back to you**.

---

## 🟢 The "DOs": What to Do

- **DO double-check facts and figures:** AI models can occasionally "hallucinate" (invent convincing-sounding facts or fake
  citations). Always cross-reference critical details, medical advice, code, or research with reliable primary sources.
- **DO check privacy settings:** Look for options to turn off chat history or opt out of having your conversations used to
  train future AI models.
- **DO use AI as a collaborator, not a substitute:** Treat AI outputs as a draft or a brainstorming partner. Apply your own
  human judgment, expertise, and voice to the final result.
- **DO stick to official and trusted tools:** Access AI platforms through official websites or verified app stores to avoid
  fake apps, phishing attempts, or malware.
- **DO verify suspicious digital content:** Be mindful that scammers use voice-cloning and deepfakes. If you receive an
  urgent or unusual request from a loved one asking for money or sensitive info, verify their identity through another
  channel.

---

## 🔴 The "DON'Ts": What NOT to Do

- **DON'T share sensitive personal info:** Never input passwords, Social Security numbers, financial details, or home
  addresses into a public chat interface.
- **DON'T paste confidential or proprietary work/school data:** Unless you are using an enterprise/campus-approved tool with
  strict data privacy guarantees, assume anything you type could end up stored or reviewed on cloud servers.
- **DON'T copy-paste output blindly:** Avoid submitting AI-generated work, code, or reports without reading, editing, and
  verifying it first. Doing so can cause errors, policy violations, or issues with plagiarism.
- **DON'T upload copyrighted or private media without permission:** Avoid uploading photos of individuals without consent,
  proprietary artwork, or protected documents into public tools.
- **DON'T rely on AI for critical medical, legal, or financial decisions:** AI does not have a real-world understanding of
  your personal context or professional credentials. Consult a qualified professional for high-stakes decisions.

---

> 💡 **Rule of Thumb:** If you wouldn't post the information on a public social media page, don't type it into a public AI
> prompt.
