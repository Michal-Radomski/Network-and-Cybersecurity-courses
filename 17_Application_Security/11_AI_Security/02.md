While both **Generative AI** and **Agentic AI** rely on advanced machine learning and Large Language Models (LLMs), they
differ fundamentally in their **purpose, autonomy, and how they interact with the world**.

Here is a breakdown of how they compare.

---

## At a Glance

| Feature               | Generative AI                                   | Agentic AI                                                   |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Primary Goal**      | Creating content (text, images, code, audio)    | Executing complex tasks and achieving goals                  |
| **Core Mechanism**    | Pattern recognition and generation              | Reasoning, planning, tool usage, and execution               |
| **Human Role**        | **Driver**: Needs detailed step-by-step prompts | **Supervisor**: Sets the goal; reviews final outcome         |
| **Autonomy**          | **Low**: Responds strictly to a single prompt   | **High**: Takes initiative, breaks down goals, self-corrects |
| **Action Capability** | Output-only (presents information or media)     | Interactive (can use APIs, browse, send emails, edit files)  |

---

## 1. Generative AI: The Creator

Generative AI focuses on **producing new content** based on patterns learned from vast training datasets. When you give it a
prompt, it predicts the most relevant next words, pixels, or code snippets.

- **How it works:** It acts as a highly knowledgeable assistant that responds directly to your instructions.
- **Key characteristic:** Passive execution. It sits idle until prompted and produces a response in a single step.
- **Example:** > You ask: _"Write a travel itinerary for a 3-day trip to Tokyo."_
  > **Generative AI** outputs a well-written 3-day itinerary as text.

---

## 2. Agentic AI: The Worker

Agentic AI builds _on top_ of Generative AI by adding **agency, decision-making, and execution capabilities**. Instead of
just generating text, an AI agent can plan multi-step workflows, interact with external software tools, evaluate its own
progress, and adjust its strategy if something goes wrong.

- **How it works:** You give it an high-level goal, and it works autonomously to accomplish it.
- **Key characteristic:** Active autonomy. It breaks a problem down into smaller tasks, uses tools (like search engines,
  calendars, or databases), and self-corrects along the way.
- **Example:**
  > You tell it: _"Book a 3-day trip to Tokyo within a $1,500 budget."_ **Agentic AI** searches flight APIs, checks hotel
  > availability, compares prices, drafts a schedule within your constraints, and prompts you for final approval to complete
  > the purchase.

---

## The Evolutionary Shift

Think of the difference like this:

> **Generative AI is like a brilliant copywriter**—you tell it exactly what to draft, and it writes it for you. **Agentic AI
> is like an executive assistant**—you give them a goal, and they figure out the steps, handle the logistics, double-check
> their work, and bring you the finished result.
