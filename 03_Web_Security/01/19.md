To balance privacy (hiding from trackers) and security (protecting from hackers) while using a browser, you should follow a
layered approach. Since we’ve discussed Manifest V3, Cookies, and SOP, here is how to put that knowledge into practice:

## 1. Choose the Right Browser

Not all browsers are equal. If privacy is your priority, your choice matters:

- Firefox: Currently the best balance. It is not moving to the restrictive Manifest V3 rules as strictly as Chrome, so ad
  blockers stay powerful. It also has "Total Cookie Protection" which isolates cookies to the site they were created on.
- Brave: Built on Chromium (like Chrome) but has a built-in "Shield" that blocks trackers and ads by default without needing
  extra extensions.
- Mullvad Browser / Tor Browser: For extreme privacy (though they can be slower for daily use).

## 2. Essential Extensions

Even a good browser needs help. Install these "must-haves":

- uBlock Origin: Still the gold standard for blocking ads and trackers. (Note: On Chrome, look for uBlock Origin Lite due to
  Manifest V3).
- Bitwarden / 1Password: Use a password manager extension. This ensures you use unique, 100+ character passwords for every
  site without needing to remember them.
- Privacy Badger: Automatically learns to block invisible trackers that follow you across the web.

## 3. Lockdown Your Settings

Most browsers ship with "convenience" settings that are bad for privacy. Change these:

- Block Third-Party Cookies: Go to settings and set this to "Always." This prevents most tracking cookies from following you
  across sites.
- Enable "HTTPS-Only Mode": This ensures the browser never sends data over unencrypted http connections.
- Disable "Save Passwords": Browsers are notoriously bad at protecting their internal password storage. Use a dedicated
  password manager instead.
- Clear on Close: Set your browser to clear its DNS cache and temporary cookies every time you close it.

## 4. Advanced "Hardening"

- DNS over HTTPS (DoH): Enable this in your browser settings (usually under Privacy/Security). It encrypts your DNS lookups
  so your ISP cannot see which websites you are visiting.
- Fingerprint Protection: Some browsers have "Strict" tracking protection that prevents "Browser Fingerprinting" (where sites
  identify you based on your screen resolution, battery level, and installed fonts).
- Containers (Firefox only): Use the "Multi-Account Containers" extension. It lets you log into two different accounts on the
  same site (like personal and work Gmail) in the same window without the cookies mixing.

## 5. Behavioral Security

- Watch the URL: Always check the domain before entering a password. Remember, SOP only protects you if you are on the
  correct site.
- Treat "Log in with Google/Facebook" with Caution: While convenient, this allows those companies to track exactly which apps
  and websites you are using. Use a standalone email/password whenever possible.
