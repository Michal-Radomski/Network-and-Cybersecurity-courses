When you are gaming, streaming, or on a video call, you want your data to travel across the internet as fast and smoothly as
possible. Two major factors that determine the quality of your connection are **latency** and **jitter**.

Here is a breakdown of what they mean and how they affect your online experience.

---

## 1. Latency: The Delay

**Latency** is the total time it takes for a packet of data to travel from its source (like your phone) to its destination
(like a gaming server) and back again. It is essentially the "ping" or delay in your connection, usually measured in
**milliseconds (ms)**.

- **The Analogy:** Think of latency like a road trip. If you drive from your house to a friend's house, latency is the total
  time you spend on the road.
- **What causes it?** Distance (how physically far away the server is), the type of connection (fiber optic vs. satellite),
  and the number of routers the data has to pass through.
- **High Latency vs. Low Latency:** \* _Low Latency (Good):_ Under 30ms. Your inputs happen instantly.
- _High Latency (Bad):_ Over 150ms. You experience noticeable "lag"—you press a button, but your character doesn't move until
  a split second later.

---

## 2. Jitter: The Consistency

**Jitter** is the variation or fluctuation in your latency over time. In a perfect world, data packets would arrive at a
perfectly steady pace. Jitter happens when packets take different amounts of time to arrive, causing an uneven flow of data.

- **The Analogy:** Imagine you and your friends are driving to the same movie theater in separate cars, leaving 2 minutes
  apart. If you all arrive exactly 2 minutes apart, your jitter is zero. If the second car gets stuck in traffic and arrives
  15 minutes late, but the third car arrives right on your bumper, that unpredictability is jitter.
- **What causes it?** Network congestion. When a router gets overloaded with data, it creates a bottleneck, forcing some
  packets to wait in line longer than others.
- **How it feels:** Jitter is the enemy of real-time communication. Even if your average latency is okay, high jitter will
  cause audio to distort, video calls to glitch/freeze, and game characters to suddenly teleport across the screen
  (rubberbanding).

---

## Summary Comparison

| Feature              | Latency                                       | Jitter                                          |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Definition**       | The time delay for data to travel round-trip. | The variation/inconsistency in that time delay. |
| **Measurement**      | Milliseconds (ms)                             | Milliseconds (ms) of variance                   |
| **Ideal Goal**       | As low as possible.                           | As close to zero as possible.                   |
| **Impact of Issues** | Constant, predictable delay (Lag).            | Choppy audio, freezing video, and stuttering.   |

To put it simply: **Latency** is about how _slow_ your connection is, while **Jitter** is about how _unstable_ your
connection is.
