A **VPN**, or **Virtual Private Network**, is a service that protects your internet connection and privacy online. It creates
a secure, encrypted "tunnel" for your data, hides your online identity by masking your IP address, and allows you to safely
use public Wi-Fi hotspots.

Think of the internet as a public highway. Normally, anyone standing by the side of the road can see your car, who is
driving, and where you are going. Using a VPN is like driving an armored, tinted limousine through a private underground
tunnel—no one can see inside, and no one knows where you originally came from.

---

## How a VPN Works (Step-by-Step)

When you turn on a VPN on your device (laptop, phone, or console), your internet traffic goes through a specific sequence:

### 1. The Request is Encrypted Locally

Before any data leaves your device, the VPN software encrypts it. Encryption turns your data (like the website you want to
visit or a password you type) into unreadable scrambled code. Even if someone intercepts it at this point, it looks like
gibberish.

### 2. Passing Through the Secure Tunnel

The encrypted data is sent from your device to your Internet Service Provider (ISP), and then straight to the **VPN server**.
To your ISP, the data just looks like encrypted traffic heading toward a single destination (the VPN server). Your ISP cannot
see which websites you are actually visiting.

### 3. Decryption at the VPN Server

Once your data reaches the VPN server, the server decrypts it. The VPN server then forwards your request to the target
website (e.g., youtube.com or google.com).

### 4. Masking Your Identity

When the target website receives the request, it sees the **VPN server's IP address and location**, not yours. Your real
location and identity remain completely hidden behind the server.

### 5. The Return Journey

When the website sends information back, the process goes in reverse: the website sends data to the VPN server $\rightarrow$
the VPN server encrypts it $\rightarrow$ it travels back through your ISP $\rightarrow$ your VPN app decrypts it on your
screen.

## Key Concepts That Make a VPN Work

A VPN relies on two main technologies to keep you safe:

- **Encryption:** This is the math used to lock your data. Most modern VPNs use **AES-256 bit encryption**, which is the same
  security standard used by banks and governments globally. It would take a supercomputer billions of years to crack.
- **Protocols:** A VPN protocol is the set of rules that determines exactly how the secure tunnel is built. Common protocols
  include **OpenVPN**, **WireGuard** (which is very fast and modern), and **IKEv2** (great for mobile devices switching
  between Wi-Fi and mobile data).

---

## Why Do People Use a VPN?

- **Privacy from ISPs:** ISPs often log your browsing history. A VPN stops them from seeing what you do online.
- **Safety on Public Wi-Fi:** Public Wi-Fi networks (like at coffee shops or airports) are notoriously insecure. Hackers on
  the same network can easily steal unencrypted data. A VPN's encryption completely prevents this.
- **Bypassing Censorship & Firewalls:** If a school or workplace network blocks certain websites, a VPN can tunnel through
  the restrictions, granting access to the open internet.
- **Changing Virtual Location:** Because you inherit the IP address of the server you connect to, you can make it look like
  you are browsing from London, Tokyo, or New York, regardless of where you actually sit.
