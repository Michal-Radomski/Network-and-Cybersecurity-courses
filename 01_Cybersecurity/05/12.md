A **Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)** attack is a type of cyberattack where a malicious actor positions themselves between two
parties—typically a user and a server—to secretly intercept, monitor, or alter the communication passing between them.

Think of it like a mail carrier opening your private letters, reading them, perhaps changing the contents, and then resealing
them before they reach the recipient. Neither you nor the recipient realizes that a third party was ever involved.

---

## How a MitM Attack Works

In a standard connection, a user communicates directly with a web server. In an MitM scenario, the attacker tricks both ends
into thinking they are talking to each other, while the attacker actually controls the entire conversation.

### The Two Main Phases:

1.  **Interception:** The attacker intercepts the data traffic before it reaches its destination. This is often done through
    compromised public Wi-Fi or by tricking a device into connecting to a rogue access point.
2.  **Decryption:** Once the traffic is intercepted, any encryption (like SSL/TLS) must be bypassed or "stripped" so the
    attacker can read the plain text data.

---

## Common Techniques

- **IP Spoofing:** The attacker masks their identity by sending packets from a "trusted" IP address, tricking the system into
  thinking it’s talking to a legitimate source.
- **ARP Spoofing:** The attacker sends fake ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) messages onto a local network to link their MAC
  address with the IP address of a legitimate server (like a default gateway).
- **DNS Spoofing (Cache Poisoning):** The attacker alters DNS records to redirect a user to a fraudulent website that looks
  identical to the real one (e.g., redirecting your bank's URL to a fake login page).
- **SSL Stripping:** The attacker forces a browser to use an unencrypted **HTTP** connection instead of the secure **HTTPS**
  version, making all data (passwords, credit card info) visible.

---

## Real-World Examples

- **Public Wi-Fi:** An attacker sets up a free hotspot named "Airport_Free_WiFi." When you connect, they can see every
  unencrypted site you visit.
- **Eavesdropping:** Stealing login credentials or session cookies to hijack a user's active social media or banking session.
- **Data Manipulation:** Intercepting a bank transfer request and changing the destination account number before the server
  receives the instruction.

---

## How to Protect Yourself

- **Look for HTTPS:** Ensure a website has the "padlock" icon in the address bar. Avoid entering sensitive data on **HTTP**
  sites.
- **Use a VPN:** A Virtual Private Network creates an encrypted tunnel for your data, making it unreadable even if
  intercepted.
- **Enable MFA:** Multi-Factor Authentication ensures that even if an attacker steals your password, they can't access your
  account without a second physical token.
- **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank or use credit cards on public networks. Use your
  mobile data instead.
- **Keep Software Updated:** Browser and OS updates often include patches for vulnerabilities that MitM attackers exploit.
