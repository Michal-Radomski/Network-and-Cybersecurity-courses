## What is Phishing?

**Phishing** is a type of cyberattack where attackers pose as a trusted entity to trick you into revealing sensitive
information. Think of it like a digital "con game"—the attacker casts a wide net (the "hook") hoping someone will bite.

Typically, the goal is to steal:

- **Login credentials** (usernames and passwords)
- **Financial data** (credit card numbers or bank account details)
- **Personal information** (Social Security numbers or addresses)

---

### How a Phishing Attack Usually Works

1.  **The Bait:** You receive a message (email, text, or DM) that looks like it’s from a legitimate source, such as your
    bank, Netflix, or even a colleague.
2.  **The Hook:** The message creates a **sense of urgency** or curiosity. For example: _"Your account has been compromised!
    Click here to verify your identity."_
3.  **The Catch:** You click a link that leads to a fake website designed to look identical to the real one. When you "log
    in," you aren't entering the real site; you are handing your password directly to the attacker.

---

### Common Types of Phishing

| Type               | Description                                                                                                             |
| :----------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Spear Phishing** | Highly targeted attacks aimed at a specific person or organization, often using personal details to seem more credible. |
| **Smishing**       | Phishing via **SMS** (text messages).                                                                                   |
| **Vishing**        | Phishing via **voice calls** or automated recordings.                                                                   |
| **Whaling**        | A spear-phishing attack specifically targeting "big fish," like CEOs or high-level executives.                          |

---

### Red Flags to Watch For

- **Generic Greetings:** Messages starting with "Dear Valued Customer" instead of your name.
- **Mismatched URLs:** Hover your mouse over a link without clicking; if the destination address looks weird or misspelled
  (e.g., `micros0ft.com` instead of `microsoft.com`), it’s a scam.
- **Urgent/Threatening Language:** Demands for immediate action to avoid "account suspension" or "legal action."
- **Unusual Attachments:** Unexpected .zip or .html files that can install malware on your device.

---

### How to Stay Safe

- **Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Even if a hacker gets your password, they can't get in without that second code.
- **Don't Click, Type:** If you get an alert from your bank, don't click the link in the email. Instead, open your browser
  and type the bank’s official URL yourself.
- **Keep Software Updated:** Security patches often fix vulnerabilities that phishers try to exploit.

> **Pro Tip:** Legitimate companies will almost **never** ask for your password, credit card number, or full Social Security
> number via email or text.
