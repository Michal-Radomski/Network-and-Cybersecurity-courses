Think of **cyber hygiene** as the digital equivalent of brushing your teeth or washing your hands. It is a set of routine
practices and habits that individuals and organizations perform to maintain the "health" and security of their devices, data,
and online identities.

Just as physical hygiene prevents illness, cyber hygiene prevents data breaches, identity theft, and malware infections.

---

## The Pillars of Good Cyber Hygiene

To keep your digital life secure, focus on these four core areas:

### 1. Identity & Access Management

- **Strong Passwords:** Use complex, unique strings for every account. Avoid common words or personal info like birthdays.
- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** This is your strongest defense. Even if a hacker gets your password, they can't get
  in without that second code from your phone or an app.
- **Password Managers:** Use tools like Bitwarden or 1Password to store and generate complex passwords so you don't have to
  remember them all.

### 2. Software Maintenance

- **Regular Updates:** Software updates aren't just for new features; they usually include "patches" for security holes.
- **Turn on Auto-Update:** Let your OS (Windows, macOS, iOS, Android) and apps update themselves overnight.
- **Delete Unused Apps:** If you don't use it, delete it. Every app is a potential entry point for a hacker.

### 3. Device Protection

- **Encryption:** Ensure your hard drive and mobile devices are encrypted so that if they are stolen, the data is unreadable.
- **Firewalls and Antivirus:** Keep your built-in firewall active and use reputable security software to scan for threats.
- **Wipe Before Disposal:** Never sell or throw away a device without performing a factory reset and data wipe.

### 4. Data Habits

- **Backups:** Follow the **3-2-1 rule**: 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types (like a hard drive and the
  cloud), with 1 copy stored off-site.
- **Phishing Awareness:** Be skeptical of urgent emails, weird links, or unexpected attachments.
- **Public Wi-Fi Caution:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or sensitive sites on public Wi-Fi unless you are using a
  **VPN** (Virtual Private Network).

---

## Why It Matters

Cyber hygiene isn't a one-time project; it’s a lifestyle. Hackers usually look for "low-hanging fruit"—people who haven't
updated their software or use the same password for everything. By practicing basic hygiene, you make yourself a much more
difficult target.

> **Quick Tip:** Start small. Pick one day a month to check for software updates and delete old accounts you no longer use.
