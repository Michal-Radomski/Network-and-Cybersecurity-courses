SDN is **software-defined networking**: a way to control and configure a network centrally in software instead of device by
device, usually by separating the control logic from the forwarding of traffic. SD-WAN is **software-defined wide area
networking**: an SDN-style approach focused on connecting branch offices, data centers, and cloud apps over WAN links with
policy-based routing, better performance, and lower cost. SDX usually means **software-defined everything** or
**software-defined x**, an umbrella term for applying the same software-driven idea to many kinds of infrastructure, such as
LAN, WAN, storage, or compute. [fortinet](https://www.fortinet.com/resources/cyberglossary/sdn-vs-sd-wan)

## How they differ

- **SDN** is the broad networking concept, often used inside data centers or enterprise networks.
  [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software-defined_networking)
- **SD-WAN** is a specific use case of SDN for wide-area networks, especially for linking distributed sites and cloud
  services. [fortinet](https://www.fortinet.com/resources/cyberglossary/sdn-vs-sd-wan)
- **SDX** is a broader umbrella term, not one single product or standard, that means “software-defined” applied to different
  infrastructure types.
  [orange-business](https://www.orange-business.com/sites/default/files/orange_apac_sd-wan_brochure_sep20.pdf)

## Simple example

If a company has a headquarters, branch offices, and cloud apps, SDN helps centrally program the network, SD-WAN helps
intelligently route traffic between sites and cloud services, and SDX is the general idea that the company can
software-control many parts of its infrastructure in a similar way.
[fortinet](https://www.fortinet.com/resources/cyberglossary/sdn-vs-sd-wan)

## One-line memory aid

- **SDN** = software-defined network control.
- **SD-WAN** = SDN for the WAN.
- **SDX** = software-defined “anything” infrastructure.
