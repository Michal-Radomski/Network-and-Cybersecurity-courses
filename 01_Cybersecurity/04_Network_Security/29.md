Honeypots and honeynets are decoy systems used to lure attackers into a controlled environment where their tactics can be
safely observed and analyzed. [1, 2]

## Honeypot (The Single Decoy)

A honeypot is a single computer system or service designed to look like a vulnerable, high-value target—such as a database or
an email server—to attract cybercriminals. [3, 4]

- Purpose: They distract attackers from real assets and act as "canaries," providing an early warning when someone
  unauthorized touches the network.
- Types by Interaction:
- Low-interaction: Emulates limited services (e.g., just a login prompt) to collect basic info with low risk and resource
  use.
  - High-interaction: Uses real operating systems and applications to let attackers stay longer, revealing complex behaviors
    and zero-day exploits.
- Types by Usage:
- Production: Deployed inside a corporate network to improve immediate security and detect active breaches.
  - Research: Used by governments or academics to study evolving threat landscapes and hacker motives. [2, 3, 5, 6, 7]

## Honeynet (The Decoy Network)

A honeynet is a collection of two or more honeypots networked together to simulate a larger, more realistic corporate
environment. [1, 8]

- Scale: It mimics entire subnets, including multiple servers, databases, and routers, to observe how attackers move
  laterally from one system to another.
- Honeywall: This is a specialized gateway or "security bridge" that monitors and limits all traffic entering and leaving the
  honeynet to ensure a compromised system cannot be used to attack outside targets.
- Advanced Tracking: Honeynets often capture deep forensic data, such as keystrokes, even if the attacker uses encrypted
  channels like SSH. [2, 5, 8, 9, 10]

## Comparison at a Glance

| Feature [1, 2, 4, 7, 8] | Honeypot                     | Honeynet                             |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Structure               | Single device or service     | Entire network of systems            |
| Complexity              | Relatively simple to deploy  | High maintenance; requires expertise |
| Main Insight            | Individual tactics and tools | Network-level lateral movement       |
| Primary Goal            | Early detection / Luring     | Deep research / Broad intelligence   |
