**Wi-Fi** is a wireless networking technology that allows devices to **connect to the internet or communicate with each other
without cables**, using radio waves.

### 📡 How Wi-Fi works

- A **router or access point** sends out a wireless signal
- Devices like phones, laptops, and tablets connect to it
- Data is transmitted through **radio frequencies** (typically 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, or 6 GHz)

---

### ⚡ Key features

- **Wireless connectivity** → no need for cables
- **Mobility** → use devices anywhere within range
- **Convenience** → easy to set up and use

---

### 📶 Wi-Fi standards (generations)

- **Wi-Fi 4 (802.11n)** → basic speeds
- **Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac)** → faster, more stable
- **Wi-Fi 6 / 6E (802.11ax)** → better performance with many devices
- **Wi-Fi 7** → newest, very high speed and low latency

---

### 🧩 Components

- **Router** → connects to the internet and broadcasts Wi-Fi
- **Access Point** → extends or creates wireless coverage
- **Client devices** → phones, laptops, smart TVs

---

### 🧠 Simple example

At home, your router creates a Wi-Fi network. Your phone connects to it, and you can browse the internet without plugging in
any cables.

---

### ⚠️ Limitations

- Signal weakens with distance and walls
- Can be affected by interference from other devices
- Usually less stable than wired connections
