Here are short, clear descriptions of other common **wireless network technologies**:

---

### 🔵 Bluetooth

- Short-range wireless technology for **device-to-device communication**
- Range: typically up to ~10–100 meters
- Low power consumption

**Used for:** headphones, keyboards, file sharing

👉 In short: **Bluetooth connects nearby devices directly without internet.**

---

### 💡 Li-Fi (Light Fidelity)

- Uses **visible light (LED bulbs)** to transmit data instead of radio waves
- Very high potential speeds
- Works only when light is visible (can’t pass through walls)

**Pros:** fast, secure **Cons:** limited range, requires line-of-sight

👉 In short: **Li-Fi sends data through light instead of radio signals.**

---

### 🛰️ Satellite networks

- Use satellites orbiting Earth to provide internet access
- Can cover **remote and rural areas** where cables aren’t available

**Pros:** global coverage **Cons:** higher latency (delay), affected by weather

👉 In short: **Satellite internet connects you via space-based systems.**

---

### 📱 Cellular networks

- Mobile networks used by smartphones (like 3G, 4G, 5G)
- Operate through **cell towers** divided into geographic areas (“cells”)

**Pros:** wide coverage, mobility **Cons:** speed depends on signal strength and network load

👉 In short: **Cellular networks provide mobile internet through towers.**

---

### 🧠 Quick comparison

- **Bluetooth** → very short range, device-to-device
- **Li-Fi** → light-based, very fast but limited
- **Satellite** → global coverage, higher delay
- **Cellular** → mobile internet over large areas
