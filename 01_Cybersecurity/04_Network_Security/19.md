**VPN (Virtual Private Network)** is a technology that allows you to **create a secure and private connection over the
internet**. It’s like having a private “tunnel” for your data when using public networks.

---

### 🔒 Key features

- **Encryption:** Data is scrambled so outsiders can’t read it
- **Authentication:** Ensures you’re connecting to a trusted server
- **Privacy:** Masks your IP address and location
- **Secure access:** Lets remote users access private networks safely

---

### ⚙️ How VPN works

1. Your device connects to a **VPN server**
2. All data is **encrypted** and sent through the VPN tunnel
3. The server forwards your traffic to the internet
4. Responses return through the same secure tunnel

---

### 🧩 Types of VPN

- **Remote Access VPN:** For individual users connecting to a company network
- **Site-to-Site VPN:** Connects entire networks (e.g., branch offices)

---

### 🧠 Simple example

When you use a VPN at a coffee shop, your laptop’s connection is encrypted. Even if someone is spying on the public Wi-Fi,
they can’t read your traffic.
