**VLAN (Virtual Local Area Network)** is a technology that allows you to **split a physical network into multiple logical
networks**. Each VLAN behaves as its own separate LAN, even if devices share the same physical switches or cables.

---

### ⚡ Key points

- **Segmentation:** Groups devices logically, not physically
- **Security:** Devices in one VLAN can be isolated from others
- **Efficiency:** Reduces network congestion by limiting broadcast traffic
- **Flexibility:** Devices can be moved between VLANs without changing physical connections

---

### 🔄 How VLAN works

- Switches are configured to assign ports to specific VLANs
- Devices in the same VLAN can communicate directly
- Communication between VLANs requires a **router or Layer 3 switch**

---

### 🧩 Example

- Office network:
  - VLAN 10 → Accounting
  - VLAN 20 → Sales
  - VLAN 30 → IT

- Employees in Accounting cannot access Sales or IT network directly

---

### 🧠 Simple analogy

Think of a **VLAN as a digital partition in your network**, like dividing a house into separate rooms: people in one room can
talk to each other, but not to people in other rooms without passing through a door (router).

---

### 📘 **What is VXLAN?**

**VXLAN (Virtual Extensible Local Area Network)** is a network technology that lets you create **large-scale virtual LANs**
over an existing IP network. It’s commonly used in **data centers and cloud environments** to connect virtual machines or
containers across different physical locations.

---

### 🔧 Why VXLAN exists

Traditional VLANs are limited to **4096** VLAN IDs. That’s not enough for large networks with many tenants (e.g., large
clouds, multi‑tenant data centers). **VXLAN expands this limit to 16 million unique network IDs**, making it much more
scalable.

---

### 🌐 How VXLAN works

- Encapsulates Ethernet frames inside **UDP/IP packets**
- Uses a **24‑bit VXLAN Network Identifier (VNI)** instead of a VLAN ID
- Packets are sent over the existing IP network (“underlay”)
- Creates a logical overlay network between endpoints

This lets devices act as if they’re on the **same LAN**, even if they’re far apart physically.

---

### 🧩 Key components

- **VTEP (VXLAN Tunnel Endpoint):** Converts between normal Ethernet frames and VXLAN encapsulated packets
- **Underlay network:** The physical IP network that carries VXLAN traffic
- **Overlay network:** The virtual network created by VXLAN

---

### 🚀 Benefits

✔ Massive scalability (millions of segments) ✔ Supports multi‑tenant environments ✔ Works over existing IP infrastructure ✔
Transparent to virtual machines and apps

---

### 🧠 Simple analogy

Think of VXLAN like putting packets **inside envelopes (UDP/IP)** so they can travel through a big postal system (IP
network), but still arrive at the right virtual neighborhood (VNI).
