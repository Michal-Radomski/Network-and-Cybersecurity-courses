### 🔌 UTP vs STP cables

#### **UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair)**

- Most common type of Ethernet cable
- Contains pairs of twisted wires (reduces interference naturally)
- **No extra shielding**
- Cheaper, lighter, easier to install
- Used in homes and offices

#### **STP (Shielded Twisted Pair)**

- Has **additional shielding** (foil or braided layer)
- Better protection against **electromagnetic interference (EMI)**
- More expensive and less flexible
- Used in environments with lots of electrical noise (factories, data centers)

👉 **In short:**

- UTP = basic, common, cheaper
- STP = protected, more durable in noisy environments

### ⚙️ What do Cat5e, Cat6, Cat7 mean?

“**Cat**” stands for **Category** — a standard that defines cable performance (speed, frequency, quality).

#### **Cat5e (Category 5e – enhanced)**

- Speed: up to **1 Gbps**
- Frequency: 100 MHz
- Suitable for most home networks

#### **Cat6 (Category 6)**

- Speed: up to **1 Gbps (100m)** or **10 Gbps (short distances)**
- Frequency: 250 MHz
- Better shielding and less interference than Cat5e

#### **Cat7 (Category 7)**

- Speed: up to **10 Gbps**
- Frequency: 600 MHz
- Strong shielding (often STP)
- Designed for high-performance networks

---

### 🧠 Simple way to understand

- **UTP vs STP** → how well the cable is protected from interference
- **Cat number** → how fast and advanced the cable is
