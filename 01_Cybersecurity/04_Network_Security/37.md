In the world of cloud computing, **IaaS**, **PaaS**, and **SaaS** represent the three main service models. The best way to
understand them is by looking at **how much of the technical "stack" you manage** versus **how much the provider manages.**

A common analogy used in the industry is **"Pizza as a Service."** You can make it at home (On-Premises), buy it frozen and
bake it (IaaS), have it delivered (PaaS), or just go to a restaurant and eat (SaaS).

---

## 1. IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)

**IaaS** is the most flexible category. The provider gives you the "raw materials"—the virtual servers, storage, and
networking—but you are responsible for installing the Operating System, managing the data, and keeping the software updated.

- **You manage:** OS, Middleware, Data, Applications.
- **Provider manages:** Physical servers, Hard drives, Networking, Virtualization (Hypervisor).
- **Real-world examples:** Amazon Web Services (AWS) EC2, Microsoft Azure VMs, Google Compute Engine.

---

## 2. PaaS (Platform as a Service)

**PaaS** is designed for developers. It removes the headache of managing servers or operating systems. You just bring your
code, and the provider gives you a "platform" where that code can run, scale, and thrive.

- **You manage:** The Application and its Data.
- **Provider manages:** Everything in IaaS, plus the OS and the software framework.
- **Real-world examples:** Heroku, Google App Engine, AWS Elastic Beanstalk.

---

## 3. SaaS (Software as a Service)

**SaaS** is what most people interact with daily. It is a complete product that is run and managed by the service provider.
You don't worry about how the service is maintained; you just use it through a web browser or app.

- **You manage:** Nothing (except your own user settings/data).
- **Provider manages:** The entire stack, from the physical cables to the app's user interface.
- **Real-world examples:** Gmail, Slack, Salesforce, Microsoft 365, Netflix.

---

## Quick Comparison Table

| Feature            | IaaS              | PaaS                | SaaS                 |
| :----------------- | :---------------- | :------------------ | :------------------- |
| **Control**        | Maximum           | Moderate            | Minimum              |
| **Setup Time**     | Slow (Build it)   | Fast (Deploy it)    | Instant (Sign up)    |
| **User Base**      | System Admins     | Developers          | End Users            |
| **Responsibility** | You secure the OS | You secure the Code | Provider secures all |

### Which one fits your needs?

- If you need a **virtual lab** to test malware (like we discussed earlier), you want **IaaS**.
- If you are **building an app** and want to launch it quickly, you want **PaaS**.
- If you just need to **send an email** or track a project, you want **SaaS**.
