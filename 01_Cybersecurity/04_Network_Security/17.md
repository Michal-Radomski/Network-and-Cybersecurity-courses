Here’s a clear comparison of **2G, 3G, 4G, and 5G cellular networks**:

---

### 📱 Overview

| Generation   | Main Use               | Speed                  | Key Features                  |
| ------------ | ---------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| **2G**       | Calls, SMS             | Very low (~kbps)       | Digital voice, text messaging |
| **3G**       | Calls + basic internet | Low (~Mbps)            | Mobile internet, video calls  |
| **4G (LTE)** | Fast internet          | High (10–100 Mbps)     | Streaming, gaming, apps       |
| **5G**       | Ultra-fast internet    | Very high (up to Gbps) | Low latency, IoT, smart tech  |

---

### 🔍 Details

#### 📞 2G (Second Generation)

- Introduced digital voice communication
- Enabled **SMS (text messages)**
- Very slow data speeds

👉 Used mainly for calls and texts

---

#### 🌐 3G (Third Generation)

- First **real mobile internet**
- Supported video calls and web browsing
- Much faster than 2G

👉 Made smartphones practical

---

#### 🚀 4G (LTE)

- High-speed internet for everyday use
- Supports HD streaming, online gaming, social media
- Much lower latency than 3G

👉 What most people still use today

---

#### ⚡ 5G (Fifth Generation)

- Extremely fast speeds (up to gigabit level)
- **Very low latency** (almost instant response)
- Can connect many devices at once

👉 Enables technologies like:

- Smart cities
- Autonomous vehicles
- Internet of Things (IoT)

---

### 🧠 Simple way to remember

- **2G** → talk & text
- **3G** → basic internet
- **4G** → fast internet
- **5G** → ultra-fast, future tech
