Protecting **Internet of Things (IoT)** devices is a unique challenge because most of these gadgets (like smart bulbs,
cameras, and thermostats) weren't built with security in mind. They often have weak processors, limited memory, and
"hardcoded" passwords that are easy for hackers to exploit.

To secure an IoT ecosystem, you need to move the defense from the device itself to the **network level**.

---

## 1. Network Segmentation (The "Guest House" Strategy)

This is the single most effective way to protect your home or office. You should put your IoT devices on a **separate
network** (VLAN) from your primary computers and phones.

- **Why?** If a hacker compromises a "smart" toaster on your guest network, they cannot "lateral move" to your main laptop to
  steal your bank passwords.
- **Action:** Use the "Guest Network" feature on your router specifically for IoT devices.

## 2. Disable UPnP (Universal Plug and Play)

UPnP is a protocol that allows devices to automatically find each other and open "holes" (ports) in your firewall to
communicate with the internet.

- **The Risk:** Hackers use UPnP to discover your devices from the outside.
- **Action:** Go into your router settings and **turn off UPnP**. You should be the one deciding which devices can talk to
  the outside world.

## 3. Change Default Credentials Immediately

Many IoT devices ship with universal usernames and passwords like `admin/admin` or `admin/password`. Bots like **Mirai**
constantly scan the internet for devices using these defaults.

- **Action:** If a device doesn't allow you to change the password, consider returning it. It is a massive security
  liability.

## 4. Manage Firmware Updates

Unlike your phone, many IoT devices don't update automatically. They sit for years with known vulnerabilities.

- **Action:** Check the manufacturer's app or website every few months for "Firmware Updates." If a company hasn't released
  an update in over a year, the device is likely "End of Life" and should be replaced.

## 5. Disable Unused Features

Most IoT devices come with features enabled by default that you don't need, such as:

- **Remote Access:** Do you really need to control your washing machine when you aren't home? If not, disable it.
- **Microphones/Cameras:** If a device has a camera or mic you don't use, mute it or cover the lens with physical tape.

---

### IoT Security Checklist

| Component          | Security Action                                                                     |
| :----------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Router**         | Change the admin password and disable WPS.                                          |
| **Connectivity**   | Use WPA3 encryption if your devices support it.                                     |
| **Cloud Accounts** | Enable **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** for the app that controls the devices. |
| **Visibility**     | Use a tool like _Fing_ to see exactly what is connected to your Wi-Fi.              |

---

### The "Golden Rule" of IoT

If a device is "Smart," it's also "Hackable." Before buying a new gadget, ask yourself: **"Does this actually need to be
connected to the internet to be useful?"** If the answer is no, keep it offline.
