Here’s the simple way to think about them: **SIEM detects and centralizes security events, SOAR automates response workflows,
and IDS watches for malicious network activity**.

## How they differ

SIEM is mainly about **visibility and correlation** across many data sources. SOAR is mainly about **action and automation**,
helping teams respond faster with less manual work. IDS is mainly about **detection at the network level**, often using
signatures or rules to flag suspicious traffic.
[exabeam](https://www.exabeam.com/explainers/siem/siem-vs-ids-key-differences-and-using-them-together/)

## How they work together

A common flow is: **IDS detects suspicious traffic, SIEM collects and correlates the alert with other logs, and SOAR
automates the response**. For example, if IDS spots a malicious connection, SIEM can confirm whether the same host also shows
abnormal logins, and SOAR can automatically block the IP or isolate the endpoint.
[splunk](https://www.splunk.com/en_us/blog/learn/siem-vs-soar.html)

## Easy analogy

- **IDS** = a security camera watching the road.
- **SIEM** = the control room that gathers feeds from many cameras and spots patterns.
- **SOAR** = the automated response team that acts on confirmed alerts.
