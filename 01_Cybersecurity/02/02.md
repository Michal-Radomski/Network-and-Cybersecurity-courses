A Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) in cybersecurity outlines procedures to restore IT systems, data, and operations after
disruptions like cyberattacks, focusing on rapid recovery to limit damage.
[online.pace](https://online.pace.edu/articles/cybersecurity/create-cybersecurity-disaster-recovery-plan/)

## Core Purpose

DRP targets technical restoration post-incident, such as ransomware or breaches, defining Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) for
downtime tolerance and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) for acceptable data loss.
[zscaler](https://www.zscaler.com/zpedia/what-is-a-disaster-recovery-plan)

## Key Components

- **Risk Assessment**: Identify cyber threats, vulnerabilities, and critical assets to prioritize recovery efforts.
  [proofpoint](https://www.proofpoint.com/us/threat-reference/disaster-recovery)
- **Backup Strategies**: Establish regular, secure backups (e.g., offsite or cloud) with tested restoration processes.
  [fortinet](https://www.fortinet.com/resources/cyberglossary/disaster-recovery)
- **Incident Response**: Detail steps for containment, eradication, and communication during and after an attack.
  [zscaler](https://www.zscaler.com/zpedia/what-is-a-disaster-recovery-plan)
- **Roles and Documentation**: Assign responsibilities and maintain audit logs for compliance and post-incident review.
  [atlassian](https://www.atlassian.com/incident-management/itsm/disaster-recovery-plan-examples)

## Implementation Steps

Conduct a Business Impact Analysis first, then set RTO/RPO metrics, develop recovery procedures, and test via simulations
quarterly. [dataguard](https://www.dataguard.com/blog/cyber-security-and-disaster-recovery-plan-best-practices/)

## DRP vs. BCP

Unlike broader Business Continuity Planning, which keeps operations running during threats, DRP activates after impact for
full system rebuild. [zscaler](https://www.zscaler.com/zpedia/what-is-a-disaster-recovery-plan)
