Incident Response (IR) in cybersecurity is a structured process for detecting, analyzing, containing, and recovering from
security breaches or attacks to minimize damage and restore normal operations. It follows standardized lifecycles like NIST
or SANS to ensure coordinated action by teams. [cynet](https://www.cynet.com/incident-response/nist-incident-response/)

## Core Purpose

IR enables rapid reaction to threats like malware infections, data breaches, or DDoS attacks, reducing downtime, data loss,
and costs while supporting legal compliance and forensic analysis.
[bluevoyant](https://www.bluevoyant.com/knowledge-center/what-is-incident-response-process-frameworks-and-tools)

## NIST Lifecycle Phases

- **Preparation**: Build an Incident Response Team (CSIRT), deploy tools like SIEM systems, and conduct training.
  [cynet](https://www.cynet.com/incident-response/nist-incident-response/)
- **Detection & Analysis**: Monitor alerts, triage events, and confirm incidents using logs and indicators of compromise
  (IoCs). [bluevoyant](https://www.bluevoyant.com/knowledge-center/what-is-incident-response-process-frameworks-and-tools)
- **Containment, Eradication & Recovery**: Isolate affected systems, remove threats, and restore from clean backups while
  monitoring for reoccurrence.
  [eccouncil](https://www.eccouncil.org/cybersecurity-exchange/incident-handling/what-is-incident-response-life-cycle/)
- **Post-Incident Activity**: Review lessons learned, update defenses, and document for future improvements.
  [xurrent](https://www.xurrent.com/blog/incident-response-lifecycle)

## Key Benefits

Effective IR cuts mean time to detect (MTTD) and respond (MTTR), enhances resilience, and integrates with BCP/DRP for
holistic cybersecurity. [pdq](https://www.pdq.com/blog/how-to-use-incident-response-lifecycle/)
