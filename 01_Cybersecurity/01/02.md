The CIA triad—Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability—forms the foundational model for information security, guiding
policies to protect data against threats.
[techtarget](https://www.techtarget.com/whatis/definition/Confidentiality-integrity-and-availability-CIA)

## Confidentiality

This principle ensures data is accessible only to authorized individuals, preventing unauthorized disclosure through measures
like encryption and access controls. Breaches occur if sensitive information, such as personal records, is exposed to
outsiders. [privacyengine](https://www.privacyengine.io/resources/glossary/cia-triad/)

## Integrity

Integrity maintains data accuracy, consistency, and trustworthiness throughout its lifecycle, protecting against unauthorized
modifications via checksums or digital signatures. For example, altering financial transaction records undermines system
reliability. [phoenixnap](https://phoenixnap.com/glossary/confidentiality-integrity-and-availability)

## Availability

Availability guarantees timely and reliable access to information and systems for authorized users, often through
redundancies like backups to counter denial-of-service attacks. System downtime, such as from hardware failures, directly
impacts operations. [sailpoint](https://www.sailpoint.com/identity-library/cia-triad)

## Overall Importance

The triad provides a balanced framework for risk management, helping organizations proactively identify vulnerabilities and
implement comprehensive defenses. It influences standards like NIST guidelines, ensuring security addresses all core aspects.
[nccoe.nist](https://www.nccoe.nist.gov/publication/1800-26/VolA/index.html)
